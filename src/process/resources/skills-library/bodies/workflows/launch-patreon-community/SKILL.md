# Launch Patreon Community

**Estimated time:** 4-8 weeks

This workflow guides you through launching a Patreon (or alternative membership
platform) from tier design through sustainable community operation. Patreon is
not just a tip jar -- it is a commitment to deliver ongoing value to people who
pay you monthly. That commitment requires thoughtful tier design, sustainable
content planning, community management, and burnout prevention strategies.

The workflow covers five phases: tier architecture, content planning, launch
execution, growth tactics, and long-term sustainability. It is designed for
creators who already have an audience (even a small one) and want to convert
free followers into paying members.

By the end of this workflow you will have: a tier structure that converts,
a content plan you can sustain, a launched Patreon with initial members, a
growth strategy for scaling, and systems to prevent creator burnout.

## Prerequisites

Before starting this workflow, ensure:

- An existing audience of any size (YouTube, podcast, newsletter, social media)
- Content that people already consume and value for free
- Willingness to commit to a regular content delivery schedule
- Understanding that membership platforms require ongoing work (this is not passive income)

## Steps

**Step 1: Design Your Tier Architecture** (uses: patreon-strategist)

design a tier structure that balances value
delivery with sustainable effort.

- Input: content type and existing audience, Current free content offering and what could be gated, Available time for creating exclusive content
- Output: Defined tiers with pricing, deliverables, and stacking logic, Delivery schedule for every reward by tier, Configured membership page with branding and descriptions
- Key focus: Analyzing your audience: what would they pay for, how much, and why?

**Step 2: Build Your Content Pipeline** (uses: content-calendar)

Use the Content Calendar and Content Monetizer skills to build a sustainable
content pipeline for membership rewards.

- Input: `tier-structure` from Step 1 (deliverables that need content), `fulfillment-matrix` from Step 1 (schedule to plan against)
- Output: 3-month editorial plan for membership content, 2-4 weeks of pre-created membership content, Reusable templates for recurring content types
- Key focus: Creating a content calendar for the first 3 months of membership content

**Step 3: Execute Your Launch** (uses: patreon-strategist)

Use the Patreon Strategist and Email Campaign skills to execute a launch that
converts existing audience into paying members.

- Input: `tier-structure` from Step 1 (offer to present), `content-backlog` from Step 2 (proof of value to showcase), `platform-setup` from Step 1 (page ready for visitors)
- Output: Day-by-day launch week activities across all channels, Announcement posts, emails, and video scripts, Conversion tracking by audience source
- Key focus: Building anticipation: tease the Patreon 2-3 weeks before launch across all

**Step 4: Grow Your Membership** (uses: community-builder)

Use the Community Builder and Personal Brand Architect skills to grow
membership through community engagement and audience expansion.

- Input: `channel-tracking` from Step 3 (effective channels to scale), `tier-structure` from Step 1 (offer to optimize), `content-calendar` from Step 2 (content driving retention)
- Output: Platform, channels, and engagement mechanics, New member welcome sequence and first-week experience, Recurring community events and member activities
- Key focus: Building community features: Discord for members, community posts, member

**Step 5: Sustain and Prevent Burnout** (uses: patreon-strategist)

Use the Patreon Strategist and Content Calendar skills to build systems for
long-term sustainability.

- Input: `fulfillment-matrix` from Step 1 (commitments to evaluate), `content-calendar` from Step 2 (workload to optimize), `growth-metrics` from Step 4 (sustainability data)
- Output: Workload assessment and optimization recommendations, Published expectations for response times and availability, Scheduled content and automated member communications
- Key focus: Auditing your fulfillment load: are you overcommitting at any tier?

## Decision Points

- **After each step:** Evaluate output quality before proceeding. If output is insufficient, iterate on the current step before moving forward.
- **Mid-workflow:** If circumstances change significantly, re-evaluate earlier steps before continuing.

## Failure Handling

- **Overcommitting on deliverables:** Promising daily content at the $5 tier is a burnout sentence. Start with less and add more based on what you can sustain.
- **Treating Patreon as a tip jar:** "Support me because you like my work" does not convert. Offer specific, tangible value at every tier.
- **Launching without a backlog:** If you launch empty and then scramble to create content, quality suffers and members churn in the first month.
- **Ignoring community:** Members who feel like a community stay longer than members who feel like customers. Invest in connection, not just content delivery.
- **Treating it as hands-off:** Membership platforms require active engagement. Posting content and never interacting with members leads to slow attrition.

## Expected Outcome

When this workflow is complete, the user will have:

1. Tier structure converts at a healthy rate (2-5% of free audience is typical)
2. Content is delivered consistently on schedule without chronic overwork
3. Community engagement creates value beyond the content itself
4. Membership grows month over month with manageable churn (under 10% monthly)
5. Revenue meets or exceeds the sustainability target
6. The creator maintains enthusiasm and avoids burnout through systems and boundaries
