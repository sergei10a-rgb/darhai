# Create Sell Digital Art

**Estimated time:** 2-4 months

This workflow guides you from developing a distinctive digital art style to
generating revenue through sales and licensing. The art market has shifted
dramatically toward digital: print-on-demand, digital downloads, NFTs,
licensing deals, and commission work create multiple income streams that did not
exist a decade ago.

The workflow covers tool mastery, style development, portfolio building,
marketplace setup, pricing, marketing, and licensing. It is designed for
artists who want to build a sustainable creative business, not just post on
social media and hope for the best.

By the end of this workflow you will have: a developed and distinctive style,
a curated portfolio, active marketplace listings, a marketing presence, a
pricing strategy, and an understanding of art licensing that opens doors to
passive income.

## Prerequisites

Before starting this workflow, ensure:

- Basic digital art skills (drawing tablet or iPad recommended)
- Digital art software (Procreate, Clip Studio Paint, Photoshop, or similar)
- A body of existing work or willingness to create 15-20 pieces for portfolio
- Understanding that building an art business takes consistent effort over months

## Decision Points

## Failure Handling

- **No distinctive style:** Selling art that looks like everyone else's makes you compete on price. Invest in developing a recognizable visual identity before opening a shop.
- **Underpricing:** New artists consistently price too low. Research what comparable artists charge and price accordingly. You can always run sales; you cannot easily raise prices.
- **Too many platforms, too little depth:** Start with 2 marketplaces and do them well. Spreading across 8 platforms with 3 listings each gets no traction.
- **Posting art without marketing context:** "Here's my new piece" gets likes. "Here's how I created this piece and you can buy a print" gets sales. Add calls to action.
- **Ignoring licensing:** Licensing is the passive income engine of the art world. A single pattern licensed for products can earn more than dozens of individual print sales.
