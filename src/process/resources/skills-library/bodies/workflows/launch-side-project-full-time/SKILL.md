# Launch Side Project Full Time

**Estimated time:** 3-9 months

This workflow guides professionals through the high-stakes transition from
employed with a side project to full-time on their own product or business.
This is one of the most consequential career decisions you can make, and most
people do it wrong -- either quitting too early with unvalidated ideas or
waiting too long and losing momentum.

The workflow follows the responsible path: validate first, build smart, prove
revenue, plan the transition, then scale. Each step has clear go/no-go
criteria so you never leap without a net. Whether you are building a SaaS
product, a consulting practice, a content business, or a physical product,
this workflow adapts to your situation.

By the end of this workflow you will have: a validated product idea, a
functioning MVP with real users, proven revenue, a financial transition plan,
and a growth strategy for your first year full-time.

## Prerequisites

Before starting this workflow, ensure:

- A side project idea (it can be at any stage from concept to live product)
- A day job providing financial stability during the validation phase
- At least 10-15 hours per week available for the side project
- Some savings or financial runway (3-6 months recommended before the leap)
- Willingness to do unglamorous validation work before building features

## Steps

**Step 1: Validate Your Idea** (uses: startup-advisor)

rigorously validate the idea before investing
more time and eventually your career in it. Most side projects fail not
because of execution but because they solve a problem nobody will pay for.

- Input: side project idea, current stage, and target customer, Any existing traction (users, revenue, waitlist, social proof), unique insight or unfair advantage in this space
- Output: Evidence from customer discovery with quotes and patterns, Market size, competition, and positioning assessment, Revenue model with pricing assumptions and projections
- Key focus: Problem validation: is this a real problem that a defined group of people

**Step 2: Build and Ship Your MVP** (uses: solopreneur-toolkit)

build and launch a minimum viable product
while maintaining your day job. The MVP must be ruthlessly scoped to what you
can ship in your available time.

- Input: `validation-results` from Step 1 (what customers actually need), `business-model` from Step 1 (what to build first to generate revenue), technical skills and available build time
- Output: Scoped MVP feature set with build timeline, Technology and tools selected for the MVP, Launch strategy with target audience and channels
- Key focus: MVP scoping: identify the smallest version that delivers core value and

**Step 3: Generate and Prove Revenue** (uses: pricing-strategist)

establish and optimize your revenue model.
Revenue is the proof that this can become a full-time business. Side project
revenue is different from startup revenue -- you need enough to replace your
salary, not enough to impress VCs.

- Input: `business-model` from Step 1 (pricing assumptions to test), `metrics-dashboard` from Step 2 (usage data to inform pricing), `mvp-spec` from Step 2 (what the product currently does)
- Output: Finalized pricing with tiers and positioning, Monthly revenue tracking with trend analysis, CAC, LTV, churn rate, and payback period
- Key focus: Pricing model selection: one-time, subscription, usage-based, freemium,

**Step 4: Plan and Execute the Transition** (uses: business-planner)

plan the transition from employment to
self-employment. This is the highest-risk step and requires careful financial
and strategic planning.

- Input: `revenue-tracker` from Step 3 (current and projected revenue), `unit-economics` from Step 3 (business health metrics), `transition-threshold` from Step 3 (financial target)
- Output: Phased plan for leaving employment with specific dates, Detailed financial projection for the transition period, Legal entity, banking, insurance, and tax preparation
- Key focus: Financial runway calculation: how many months of expenses can you cover

**Step 5: Scale the Business** (uses: productivity-system-builder)

establish the systems and habits
needed to run a one-person business full-time. The transition from side project
hours to full-time is not just "more hours" -- it requires fundamentally
different productivity systems.

- Input: `growth-roadmap` from Step 4 (first 90 days full-time plan), `revenue-tracker` from Step 3 (revenue to grow from), `unit-economics` from Step 3 (metrics to improve)
- Output: Structured daily routine and productivity system, Prioritized growth levers with execution plan, Tasks identified for automation with tools selected
- Key focus: Time structure: creating a productive daily routine without external

## Decision Points

- **After each step:** Evaluate output quality before proceeding. If output is insufficient, iterate on the current step before moving forward.
- **Mid-workflow:** If circumstances change significantly, re-evaluate earlier steps before continuing.

## Failure Handling

- **Building before validating:** The most common failure. You spend months building something nobody will pay for. Validate first with conversations and pre-sales.
- **Quitting too early:** Do not quit your job on an idea. Quit on proven revenue with a financial runway.
- **Quitting too late:** Analysis paralysis can keep you employed forever. Set a clear transition threshold and honor it.
- **Underestimating the loneliness:** Going from a team environment to solo work is a major adjustment. Plan for community and social interaction.
- **Not setting up business basics:** Taxes, insurance, and legal structure are boring but ignoring them creates expensive problems.

## Expected Outcome

When this workflow is complete, the user will have:

1. The idea is validated with customer evidence before significant investment
2. An MVP is shipped and used by real customers
3. Revenue is generated and trending upward over at least 3 months
4. The transition from employment is planned with financial safety nets
5. Full-time operations are structured with sustainable productivity systems
6. The business is growing and on a path to replacing previous income
7. A fallback plan exists and is never needed
