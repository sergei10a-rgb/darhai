## Understanding Grades

```
BOULDERING GRADES:
V-Scale (US)  | Font Scale | Difficulty Description
--------------+------------+------------------------------
VB-V0         | 3-4        | Beginner: large holds, simple movement
V1-V2         | 4+-5       | Developing: smaller holds, basic technique
V3-V4         | 5+-6A      | Intermediate: requires technique and strength
V5-V6         | 6B-7A      | Advanced: specific training needed
V7-V8         | 7A+-7B+    | Expert: years of dedicated training
V9+           | 7C+        | Elite: exceptional talent and training

ROUTE GRADES (Yosemite Decimal System):
YDS Grade     | Description                    | Typical Climber Level
--------------+--------------------------------+---------------------
5.5-5.7       | Beginner: jugs, big footholds  | First month
5.8-5.9       | Novice: technique matters      | 3-6 months
5.10a-5.10d   | Intermediate: real climbing    | 6-18 months
5.11a-5.11d   | Advanced: dedicated training   | 2-5 years
5.12a-5.12d   | Expert: serious athlete        | 5+ years dedicated
5.13+         | Elite: exceptional level       | Lifetime pursuit

IMPORTANT: Grades vary wildly between areas and gyms.
Gym grades are typically 1-3 grades softer than outdoor.
A "gym 5.11" climber is often a "crag 5.9-5.10a" climber.
Focus on skill development, not grade chasing.
```

## Movement Fundamentals

### The Four Principles

```
PRINCIPLE 1: FEET FIRST
- Look at footholds before hand holds
- 70% of climbing movement should come from legs
- Place feet precisely on the best part of the hold
- Trust your feet (rubber friction is remarkable)
- Quiet feet = good technique (no scraping or slipping)

PRINCIPLE 2: STRAIGHT ARMS
- Hang from straight arms whenever possible
- Bent arms fatigue biceps rapidly (the "pump")
- Straight arms transfer weight to skeleton, not muscles
- Rest positions: straight arms, weight on feet, shake out hands

PRINCIPLE 3: HIPS TO WALL
- Keep your center of gravity close to the wall
- Hips close to wall = more weight on feet = less arm strain
- Turn sideways (flagging) to reach further with less effort
- Drop a knee to bring hips in on steep terrain

PRINCIPLE 4: MOVE WITH INTENTION
- Read the route before climbing (visualize the sequence)
- Move decisively - hesitation wastes energy
- Breathe (people hold their breath when stressed)
- Rest before you need to, not after you are pumped
```

### Essential Techniques

```
FOOTWORK:
- Edging: Using the inside edge of shoe on small footholds
- Smearing: Pressing rubber flat against featureless rock (friction)
- Heel hook: Hooking heel on hold, pulling with hamstring
- Toe hook: Hooking toe behind/under a feature for tension
- Backstep: Turning sideways, using outside edge of shoe
- Drop knee: Rotating hips, knee drops down (power + reach)

HANDHOLDS:
- Open hand: Fingers extended over hold (best for endurance)
- Half crimp: Fingers at 90 degrees, thumb optional (power/safety balance)
- Full crimp: Fingers at 90 degrees with thumb locked (max power, injury risk)
- Pinch: Squeezing hold between thumb and fingers
- Sloper: Palm and friction dependent (engage shoulders, keep hips in)
- Pocket: One or more fingers in a hole (careful - high finger strain)

BODY POSITIONS:
- Flagging: Extending one leg out for balance (reduces barn-dooring)
- Stemming: Pushing outward on two facing surfaces
- Mantling: Pressing down to get body over a ledge
- Lock-off: Holding one arm in position while reaching with the other
```

## Gear Essentials

```
STARTING GEAR (Bouldering):
- Climbing shoes (snug but not painful, beginner: flat-lasted)
- Chalk bag and chalk (or liquid chalk for gyms)
- Crash pad (outdoor bouldering)
Total: $150-300

STARTING GEAR (Top-Rope Gym):
- Climbing shoes
- Harness (try on several, comfort matters most)
- Belay device (assisted-braking recommended for beginners)
- Locking carabiner (HMS/pear shape for belay device)
- Chalk bag
Total: $200-400

OUTDOOR SPORT CLIMBING (add to above):
- Helmet (non-negotiable outdoors)
- Quickdraws (12-16 for most single-pitch routes)
- 60-70m dynamic rope (single-rated)
- Personal anchor system or sling with locker
Total: $600-1000 additional

SHOE SELECTION:
Beginner:     Flat-lasted, comfortable, moderate rubber
Intermediate: Slight downturn, snug fit, stickier rubber
Advanced:     Aggressive downturn, performance fit

FIT GUIDE: Shoes should be snug with toes slightly curled, not painfully.
No dead space. New shoes stretch 0.5-1 size. Synthetic less than leather.
```

## Safety Systems

### Belaying

```
BELAY FUNDAMENTALS (never skip this):
1. Learn from a certified instructor (not YouTube alone)
2. Practice until belaying is automatic, not conscious
3. ALWAYS: brake hand stays on the rope below the device
4. NEVER: let go of the brake strand for any reason

PBUS METHOD (Pay-Brake-Under-Slide):
1. PAY out or take in slack
2. BRAKE hand pulls rope down to brake position
3. UNDER hand slides under brake hand
4. SLIDE brake hand back up to device
Repeat. This keeps a hand on brake strand at all times.

COMMUNICATION COMMANDS:
Climber: "On belay?"     Belayer: "Belay on."
Climber: "Climbing."     Belayer: "Climb on."
Climber: "Slack!"        Belayer gives slack
Climber: "Up rope!"      Belayer takes in slack
Climber: "Take!"         Belayer takes in and locks off
Climber: "Falling!"      Belayer braces for soft catch
Climber: "Off belay."    Belayer: "Belay off."
```

### Anchor Fundamentals

```
ANCHOR CRITERIA (EARNEST):
E - Equalized: load shared between points
A - Angles: keep below 60 degrees between legs
R - Redundant: at minimum 2 independent points
N - No Extension: if one point fails, minimal shock load
E - Efficient: simple to build, quick to assess
S - Strong: each point must hold expected loads
T - Timely: built quickly, appropriate for the situation

CLIPPING QUICKDRAWS (Sport Leading):
- Clip with rope running from wall-side through draw to climber-side
- Clip at waist to chest height (not overhead)
- Practice clipping on the ground until automatic
- Learn to clip with both hands
```

## Training Plan

### Beginner (Months 1-6)

```
WEEKLY SCHEDULE:
- Climb 2-3 days per week (with rest days between)
- Focus: technique over strength
- Every session: 15 min warm-up, climb, 10 min stretch

SESSION STRUCTURE:
Warm-up (15 min):
  - 5 min light cardio, joint circles
  - 3-4 easy routes well below your limit

Technique Block (30-45 min):
  - Climb routes focusing on ONE technique per session
  - Week 1-2: Feet (quiet, precise placement)
  - Week 3-4: Straight arms (rest positions)
  - Week 5-6: Flagging and hip positioning
  - Repeat cycle with increasing difficulty

Volume Block (20-30 min):
  - Many routes at moderate difficulty
  - Focus on movement flow and efficiency

Cool-down (10 min):
  - Stretching (forearms, shoulders, hips, fingers)
  - Antagonist exercises: push-ups, shoulder press
```

### Intermediate (Months 6-18)

```
ADD TO YOUR TRAINING:
- Hangboard: Dead hangs on large edges to start
  7 sec on, 3 sec off, 6 reps, 3 sets
  Increase difficulty with smaller edges, not added weight
  NEVER full-crimp on a hangboard (injury risk)

- Core: Hollow body holds, hanging leg raises, plank variations

- Antagonist training: Push-ups, shoulder press, reverse wrist curls

- Mental skills: Practice falling on lead in controlled settings
  Take intentional falls from safe positions
  Build comfort with falling incrementally
```

### Bouldering vs Lead Climbing

```
BOULDERING:
- Short problems (4-8 moves), high intensity
- No rope, crash pads for protection
- Emphasizes power, problem-solving, technique
- Lower time commitment, social atmosphere
- Risk: ground falls (ankle injuries most common)

LEAD CLIMBING (Sport):
- Full routes, sustained effort, 15-30m
- Rope protection, clip quickdraws as you climb
- Emphasizes endurance, route reading, mental game
- Requires a competent belayer
- Risk: longer falls, requires solid safety skills

PROGRESSION PATH:
Gym bouldering -> Gym top-rope -> Gym lead -> Outdoor top-rope -> Outdoor lead
Each transition requires new skills. Do not skip steps.
```

## Climbing Ethics and Etiquette

```
OUTDOOR ETHICS:
- Leave no trace: pack out ALL trash including tape and chalk
- Respect access: follow local rules, park legally, be quiet
- Yield: whoever is on the route has right of way
- Do not alter rock: no chipping, gluing, or manufacturing holds
- Minimize chalk: brush holds after use on boulders
- Wildlife: respect nesting season closures

GYM ETIQUETTE:
- Do not walk under climbers
- Take turns on popular routes
- Keep beta (advice) to yourself unless asked
- Be encouraging - climbing culture is generally welcoming
```

## Injury Prevention

```
COMMON INJURIES:
- Finger pulley strains/tears (A2 pulley most common)
- Shoulder impingement
- Elbow tendinitis
- Skin flappers and abrasions

PREVENTION:
1. Warm up thoroughly every session (not optional)
2. Avoid full crimping (half crimp or open hand instead)
3. Do antagonist exercises (push muscles balance pull muscles)
4. Rest between climbing days (tendons adapt slower than muscles)
5. Listen to pain - sharp finger pain means STOP immediately
6. Gradual volume increase (no more than 10-15% per week)
7. Stretch forearms, shoulders, and hips after every session
```
