## Questions to Ask First

Before setting goals for the new year, reflect honestly:

- What were your goals last year, and how did they go?
- What accomplishments from this year are you most proud of?
- What area of your life feels most in need of attention (health, career, relationships, finances, personal growth)?
- What has prevented you from achieving goals in the past?
- How much time and energy can you realistically dedicate to new pursuits?
- Do you prefer big ambitious goals or small consistent habits?
- What does a successful year look like to you in specific terms?
- Who in your life could support your goals?
- What are you willing to give up to make room for what you want?
- Have you tried accountability systems before, and what worked or failed?

## Goal-Setting Frameworks

### The SMART+ Framework

Each goal should pass this test:

| Element | Question | Your Answer |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Specific | What exactly will you accomplish? | |
| Measurable | How will you track progress? | |
| Achievable | Is this realistic given your resources? | |
| Relevant | Does this align with your values and priorities? | |
| Time-bound | What is your deadline or timeline? | |
| + Exciting | Does this goal energize you? | |
| + Controllable | Is the outcome within your control? | |

### The Three-Goal Rule

Instead of a long list of resolutions, choose a maximum of three primary goals:

1. **One growth goal** - Something that pushes you to expand your capabilities
2. **One maintenance goal** - Something that protects what you have already built
3. **One exploration goal** - Something new you want to try without pressure to master

## Common Goal Categories and Approaches

### Health and Fitness

**Instead of:** "Get in shape"
**Try:** "Exercise for 30 minutes, 4 days per week, tracking with a habit app"

| Approach | Weekly Time | Difficulty | Best For |
|----------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Walking habit | 2-3 hours | Low | Beginners, sustainability |
| Home workouts | 3-4 hours | Medium | Busy schedules, privacy |
| Gym membership | 4-6 hours | Medium-High | Structure, variety |
| Sports/classes | 2-4 hours | Medium | Social motivation |
| Training program | 5-8 hours | High | Specific fitness goals |

### Financial Goals

**Instead of:** "Save more money"
**Try:** "Save $5,000 by December 31 by automatically transferring $420/month to savings"

| Goal Type | Monthly Action | Annual Impact |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| Emergency fund | Auto-save fixed amount | 3-6 months expenses |
| Debt payoff | Extra payments + strategy | Significant principal reduction |
| Investing start | Open account, auto-invest | Building wealth habit |
| Spending reduction | Track and cut specific categories | Hundreds to thousands saved |
| Income increase | Skill building, side project | New income stream |

### Career and Professional

**Instead of:** "Advance my career"
**Try:** "Earn a specific certification by June and apply for two senior roles by September"

- Identify the specific position or milestone you want
- Map the skills gap between where you are and where you want to be
- Find one mentor or professional connection to nurture monthly
- Dedicate specific weekly hours to skill development
- Track applications, networking contacts, and learning progress

### Personal Growth

**Instead of:** "Read more"
**Try:** "Read 24 books this year (2 per month) by reading 20 minutes before bed each night"

- Choose a specific skill or area of knowledge to develop
- Set a measurable reading, learning, or practice target
- Join a community of others pursuing similar growth
- Schedule learning time like an appointment
- Apply what you learn through projects or teaching others

### The Weekly Review System

Set aside 20-30 minutes each week (same day and time) to answer:

1. **What did I accomplish this week toward my goals?**
2. **What got in the way, and how can I address it?**
3. **What are my three most important actions for next week?**
4. **Am I still excited about this goal? If not, why?**
5. **Do I need to adjust my approach (not my goal)?**

### The Monthly Check-In

At the end of each month, do a deeper review:

- [ ] Review progress against monthly targets
- [ ] Update your tracking metrics
- [ ] Celebrate wins (even small ones)
- [ ] Identify patterns in what helps and what hinders
- [ ] Adjust next month's targets if needed
- [ ] Reconnect with your "why" for each goal
- [ ] Share progress with accountability partner

### The Quarterly Reset

Every three months, do a mini version of the annual review:

- Are your goals still relevant and exciting?
- Is the pace sustainable?
- Do any goals need to be modified, paused, or replaced?
- What has been your biggest lesson this quarter?
- What is one thing you will do differently next quarter?

## Overcoming Common Obstacles

### When Motivation Fades (Usually Week 3-4)

- Revisit your "why" - write down the reason this goal matters
- Lower the bar temporarily - do the minimum viable version
- Change the environment - try a different time, place, or method
- Connect with someone who shares the goal
- Review your progress from day one - you have come further than you think
- Accept that motivation is unreliable; systems are what matter

### When Life Disrupts Your Plans

- Expect disruptions - they are not failures, they are normal
- Have a "minimum viable habit" for stressful periods
- Build buffer weeks into your timeline
- Separate missing one day from quitting entirely
- Resume where you left off without guilt or starting over

### When You Want to Quit

Before abandoning a goal, ask:
- Am I quitting because it is genuinely wrong for me, or because it is hard right now?
- Would I regret quitting in six months?
- Can I modify the goal instead of abandoning it?
- Am I comparing my progress to someone else's instead of my own?
- Have I given this enough time to see results (minimum 8-12 weeks)?

## New Year Transition Ritual

### A Simple Process for Closing One Year and Opening Another

1. **Review** - Complete the four-part reflection above
2. **Release** - Write down what you are leaving behind; symbolically let it go
3. **Envision** - Spend 15 minutes imagining your ideal December 31 next year
4. **Commit** - Choose your three goals and write them somewhere visible
5. **Begin** - Take one small action toward each goal on January 1
6. **Share** - Tell one person about your intentions
