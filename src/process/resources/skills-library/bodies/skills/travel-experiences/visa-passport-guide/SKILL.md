# Visa and Passport Guide

## When to Use

**Use this skill when:**
- User needs guidance on passport application or renewal process
- User wants to understand visa requirements for a specific destination
- User needs help organizing travel documents for international travel
- User wants to know processing times and expedited options for passports

**Do NOT use this skill when:**
- User needs immigration legal advice -- refer to immigration attorney
- User wants to apply for residency or citizenship -- beyond travel document scope
- User needs real-time visa policy updates -- check official embassy websites

## Process

1. **Step 1:** Determine travel document needs: passport status, destination visa requirements
2. **Step 2:** Check processing timelines against travel dates
3. **Step 3:** Identify required documents and application procedures
4. **Step 4:** Create document preparation checklist with deadlines
5. **Step 5:** Provide backup plan for delays or complications

## Purpose

This skill helps travelers navigate the complex world of travel documentation -- passports, visas, travel insurance, vaccination requirements, and entry restrictions. Proper documentation is the foundation of stress-free international travel.

---

## Questions to Ask the User First

1. **What is your nationality?** (determines visa requirements)
2. **Where are you traveling?** (destination country/countries)
3. **When are you traveling?** (timeline affects processing urgency)
4. **How long will you stay?** (duration affects visa type)
5. **What is the purpose of travel?** (tourism, business, study, work, transit)
6. **Do you currently have a valid passport?** If yes, when does it expire?
7. **Have you been denied a visa before?**
8. **Are you visiting multiple countries?** (may need multiple visas)
9. **Do you have travel insurance?**
10. **Are there any health/vaccination requirements you are aware of?**

## Step 1: Passport Application and Renewal

### Passport Timeline (US Citizens)
```
US PASSPORT TIMELINE:

ROUTINE PROCESSING:
- New passport: 6-8 weeks
- Renewal: 4-6 weeks
- Cost: $130 (new adult book), $30 renewal by mail

EXPEDITED PROCESSING:
- Processing time: 2-3 weeks
- Additional cost: +$60
- Must request at time of application

URGENT / EMERGENCY:
- Life-or-death emergency: Same day at regional passport agency
- Urgent travel (within 2 weeks): Appointment at passport agency
- Must have proof of travel (booked tickets)
- Cost: $60 expediting fee + $60 agency fee

PASSPORT CARD:
- Valid for: Land and sea travel to Canada, Mexico, Caribbean, Bermuda only
- NOT valid for international air travel
- Cost: $30 (new), less for renewal
- Good to have as backup ID

APPLICATION CHECKLIST:
[ ] Completed Form DS-11 (new) or DS-82 (renewal by mail)
[ ] Proof of citizenship (birth certificate, previous passport)
[ ] Valid photo ID
[ ] Passport photo (2x2 inches, white background, recent)
[ ] Payment (check or money order for mail; card accepted in person)
[ ] Current passport (for renewal -- will be returned)
```

### Key Passport Rules
```
PASSPORT VALIDITY RULES:

THE 6-MONTH RULE:
Many countries require your passport to be valid for at least
6 months BEYOND your planned departure date from their country.

Countries with 6-month requirement include:
- China, Japan, Thailand, Indonesia, Vietnam
- Turkey, Saudi Arabia, UAE
- Brazil, Ecuador, Kenya, South Africa
- Many others

Countries that do NOT require 6 months:
- Most of Europe (Schengen area -- 3 months past departure)
- UK (valid for duration of stay)
- Canada (valid for duration of stay)
- Always verify for your specific destination

BLANK PAGES:
- Some countries require 1-2 blank pages for entry/exit stamps
- Visa applications often require a blank page
- If running low, renew your passport

PASSPORT PHOTO REQUIREMENTS (US):
[ ] 2 x 2 inches (51 x 51 mm)
[ ] White or off-white background
[ ] Taken within last 6 months
[ ] Full face, front view, eyes open
[ ] No glasses (as of 2016)
[ ] No headwear (religious exceptions allowed)
[ ] Neutral expression
```

## Step 2: Visa Requirements

### Visa Requirement Lookup Process
```
VISA RESEARCH WORKFLOW:

Step 1: Identify your passport nationality
Step 2: Identify your destination country
Step 3: Check visa requirements:

PRIMARY SOURCES (use official sources, not blogs):
[ ] Destination country's embassy/consulate website
[ ] Your country's State Department/Foreign Affairs travel page
    - US: travel.state.gov
    - UK: gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice
    - Canada: travel.gc.ca
    - Australia: smartraveller.gov.au
[ ] IATA TravelCentre (used by airlines): iatatravelcentre.com
[ ] Sherpa (visa and entry requirement tool)

Step 4: Determine visa type needed:
- Visa-free / visa exemption
- Visa on arrival (VOA)
- Electronic visa (eVisa)
- Pre-arranged visa (must apply before travel)
- Transit visa (for layovers)

Step 5: Check additional entry requirements:
- Return/onward ticket required?
- Proof of accommodation?
- Proof of sufficient funds?
- Vaccination requirements?
- Travel insurance required?
```

### Visa Types Explained
```
VISA TYPE GUIDE:

VISA-FREE:
- No visa needed for short stays (usually 30-90 days)
- Just show up with a valid passport
- Common for: US/EU citizens in many countries
- Still need to check passport validity requirements

VISA ON ARRIVAL (VOA):
- Obtain visa at the border/airport
- Usually pay a fee in cash (bring USD or EUR)
- May need passport photos
- Can have long lines at busy airports
- Tip: Have exact change and photos ready

ELECTRONIC VISA (eVisa):
- Apply online before travel
- Usually processed in 1-7 days
- Print the confirmation
- Examples: Turkey, India, Australia (ETA), Kenya, Cambodia

EMBASSY/CONSULATE VISA:
- Must apply in person or by mail before travel
- Processing: 1-4 weeks typical
- May require interview
- Examples: China, Russia, Brazil (for some nationalities)

TRANSIT VISA:
- Required for layovers in some countries
- Even if you do not leave the airport
- Check: China (72/144-hour transit visa exemption for some),
  UAE, UK, Schengen area
- Book layovers carefully to avoid surprise visa needs
```

### Visa Application Tips
```
VISA APPLICATION CHECKLIST:

STANDARD DOCUMENTS (most visa applications require):
[ ] Valid passport (6+ months validity, blank pages)
[ ] Completed application form
[ ] Passport-size photos (check specific requirements)
[ ] Proof of travel (flight booking or itinerary)
[ ] Proof of accommodation (hotel booking or invitation letter)
[ ] Proof of financial means (bank statements, 3-6 months)
[ ] Travel insurance (some countries require specific minimums)
[ ] Cover letter explaining purpose of visit
[ ] Employment verification letter
[ ] Return/onward ticket
[ ] Visa fee payment

BUSINESS VISA ADDITIONAL:
[ ] Invitation letter from host company
[ ] Company letter stating purpose of travel
[ ] Business registration documents

STUDENT VISA ADDITIONAL:
[ ] Acceptance letter from educational institution
[ ] Proof of tuition payment
[ ] Academic transcripts
[ ] Language proficiency test results

APPLICATION TIPS:
1. Apply early (at least 4-6 weeks before travel)
2. Follow instructions EXACTLY (forms, photos, documents)
3. Do not leave any fields blank (write "N/A" if not applicable)
4. Be honest about travel history and previous visa rejections
5. Provide more documentation than requested (shows strong ties to home)
6. For Schengen visas, apply at the embassy of your MAIN destination
7. For transit visas, check if your layover airport requires one
8. Book refundable flights/hotels until visa is approved
9. Keep copies of everything you submit
10. Track your application online if the system allows it
```

## Step 3: Travel Insurance Selection

### Travel Insurance Decision Framework
```
TRAVEL INSURANCE GUIDE:

DO YOU NEED IT? YES if any of these apply:
[ ] International travel
[ ] Trip cost exceeds $2,000
[ ] Pre-existing medical conditions
[ ] Adventure activities planned
[ ] Traveling during hurricane/monsoon season
[ ] Non-refundable bookings
[ ] Destinations with expensive medical care

TYPES OF COVERAGE:

1. TRIP CANCELLATION/INTERRUPTION
   Covers: Non-refundable costs if trip is cancelled or cut short
   Look for: Covered reasons (illness, weather, job loss)
   Best option: "Cancel for Any Reason" (CFAR) -- pays 75% for any reason

2. MEDICAL/EVACUATION
   Covers: Emergency medical care abroad, medical evacuation
   Minimum recommended: $100,000 medical, $250,000 evacuation
   Critical for: Countries with expensive healthcare (US, Japan, Europe)
   Note: Your domestic health insurance usually does NOT cover you abroad

3. BAGGAGE/PERSONAL ITEMS
   Covers: Lost, stolen, or delayed luggage
   Usually: $500-$2,000 per person
   Check: Your credit card may already cover this

4. TRAVEL DELAY
   Covers: Hotel, meals, and essentials during unexpected delays
   Usually: $100-$300/day with a cap
```

## Step 4: Vaccination Requirements

### Vaccination Research Process
```
VACCINATION CHECKLIST:

Step 1: CHECK REQUIREMENTS
[ ] CDC Travelers' Health: wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel
[ ] WHO country health profiles
[ ] Destination country's entry requirements

Step 2: COMMON TRAVEL VACCINATIONS

REQUIRED BY SOME COUNTRIES:
- Yellow Fever: Required for entry in parts of Africa and South America.
  Must have International Certificate of Vaccination (Yellow Card).
  Some countries require proof if arriving from an endemic country.

RECOMMENDED (depending on destination):
- Hepatitis A: Recommended for most developing countries (food/water)
- Hepatitis B: If exposure to blood/body fluids possible
- Typhoid: South Asia, parts of Africa, Central/South America
- Japanese Encephalitis: Rural Asia
- Rabies: If significant animal exposure expected
- Malaria prophylaxis: Sub-Saharan Africa, Southeast Asia, Amazon basin
  (not a vaccine -- prescription medication)
- Meningitis: Sub-Saharan "meningitis belt," Hajj requirement

ROUTINE (ensure up to date):
- MMR (measles, mumps, rubella)
- Tetanus/Diphtheria (Tdap booster every 10 years)
- Flu (seasonal)
- COVID-19 (check destination requirements)
- Polio (booster if traveling to endemic areas)

Step 3: SCHEDULE APPOINTMENTS
- Visit a travel clinic 4-6 weeks before departure
- Some vaccinations require multiple doses over time
- Yellow Fever vaccination must be at an authorized center
- Keep your vaccination card with your passport
```

## Step 5: Entry Restriction Awareness

### Common Entry Requirements Beyond Visa
```
ENTRY REQUIREMENT CHECKLIST:

[ ] Passport validity (6 months beyond stay in many countries)
[ ] Blank passport pages (1-2 typically required)
[ ] Visa or entry permit
[ ] Return or onward ticket (many countries require proof you will leave)
[ ] Proof of accommodation (hotel booking, host letter)
[ ] Proof of sufficient funds (bank statement, credit cards, cash)
[ ] Travel insurance (required by Schengen, Cuba, some others)
[ ] Vaccination certificate (Yellow Fever, COVID, others)
[ ] Customs declaration form (filled out on plane)
[ ] Immigration arrival card (some countries still use paper forms)
[ ] Biometric registration (fingerprints, photo at immigration)

SPECIAL SITUATIONS:
- Dual nationality: Some countries do not recognize dual citizenship.
  Enter on the passport of that country if you hold one.
- Passport stamps: Israeli stamps can cause issues entering some
  countries (Israel now uses entry cards, not stamps).
- Overstay penalties: Overstaying a visa can result in fines,
  deportation, and future entry bans.
- Criminal record: Many countries (US, Canada, Australia, Japan)
  can deny entry to travelers with criminal records.
```

## Step 6: Document Copies Strategy

### The 3-2-1 Document Strategy
```
DOCUMENT BACKUP SYSTEM:

THE 3-2-1 RULE:
3 copies of every important document
2 different storage methods
1 copy left at home with a trusted person

PHYSICAL COPIES (carry separately from originals):
[ ] Passport photo page
[ ] Visa(s)
[ ] Travel insurance policy and emergency number
[ ] Flight confirmations
[ ] Hotel confirmations
[ ] Emergency contacts
[ ] Credit card numbers and bank phone numbers
[ ] Prescriptions (generic drug names)

DIGITAL COPIES:
[ ] Scan or photo everything above
[ ] Store in cloud (Google Drive, iCloud, Dropbox)
[ ] Email copies to yourself
[ ] Store in a password-protected folder on your phone
[ ] Consider a secure travel app (1Password, TripIt)

HOME COPIES:
[ ] Leave copies of passport, visa, itinerary with trusted
    person at home
[ ] Share your live location with someone (Google Maps, Find My)
[ ] Leave emergency contact information for your trusted person
```

```

Passport + embassy visa + insurance + invitation/booking + financial proof + interview

VERY COMPLEX (multiple visas, transit visas):
Multi-country trips through various visa regimes
Each country's requirements checked individually + transit visa requirements
```

## Edge Cases

- **Incomplete information:** Ask clarifying questions before proceeding. Do not assume details the user has not provided.
- **Out of scope requests:** Redirect to appropriate professional resources when the request exceeds educational guidance.
- **Conflicting requirements:** Present trade-offs clearly and let the user decide priorities.
