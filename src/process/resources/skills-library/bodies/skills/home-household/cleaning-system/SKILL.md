# Cleaning System Designer
## When to Use

**Use this skill when:**
- User asks about cleaning system
- User needs guidance on cleaning system topics
- User wants a structured approach to cleaning system

**Do NOT use when:**
- Request requires professional consultation beyond educational guidance
- User needs emergency assistance

## Questions to Ask the User First

1. **How many people live in your home?** (Adults, children, pets -- all generate different messes)
2. **Home size?** (Rooms, square footage, number of bathrooms)
3. **What's your current cleaning situation?** (Regular routine, sporadic, overwhelmed, starting from scratch)
4. **How much time can you dedicate daily?** (15 min, 30 min, 1 hour)
5. **Are there specific problem areas?** (Kitchen always messy, bathrooms need help, etc.)
6. **Who will participate in cleaning?** (Just you, partner, whole family, cleaning service for some tasks)
7. **Cleaning product preferences?** (Commercial, natural/DIY, fragrance-free, specific brands)
8. **Any health considerations?** (Allergies, asthma, chemical sensitivities, physical limitations)
9. **Pets?** (Type, shedding level, indoor/outdoor)
10. **Flooring types?** (Hardwood, carpet, tile, laminate -- affects cleaning methods)
11. **Current cleaning supplies you own?**

### DIY Natural Cleaner Recipes
```
ALL-PURPOSE CLEANER:
  1 cup water
  1 cup white vinegar
  10-15 drops essential oil (optional: lemon, tea tree, lavender)
  Combine in spray bottle. Shake before use.
  Use on: counters, appliances, tile, glass, most surfaces
GLASS CLEANER:
  1 cup water
  1 cup rubbing alcohol (or white vinegar)
  1 tablespoon white vinegar
  Combine in spray bottle.
  Spray, wipe with lint-free cloth or newspaper.
BATHROOM SCRUB:
  1/2 cup baking soda
  Enough dish soap to make a paste
  10 drops tea tree oil (natural antifungal)
  Apply to tub, tile, grout. Scrub with brush. Rinse.
TOILET BOWL CLEANER:
  1/2 cup baking soda
  1/4 cup white vinegar
  Sprinkle baking soda in bowl, add vinegar.
  Let fizz 10 minutes. Scrub with toilet brush. Flush.
WOOD FLOOR CLEANER:
  1 gallon warm water
  1/4 cup white vinegar
  Damp mop only (never wet -- excess water damages wood).
  For sealed hardwood only. Not for waxed floors.
GRANITE/STONE CLEANER:
  2 cups water
  1/4 cup rubbing alcohol
  3 drops dish soap
  Spray and wipe. Vinegar is too acidic for stone.
STAINLESS STEEL:
  Wipe with damp microfiber cloth in the direction of the grain.
  Polish with a few drops of mineral oil on clean cloth.
DISINFECTANT (when actually needed):
  disinfectant following label contact time requirements.
  Disinfect: after raw meat handling, illness in household, bathroom
```

## Cleaning Schedules

### Daily Cleaning (15-20 Minutes)
```
DAILY RESET (do every day, non-negotiable):

KITCHEN (5-7 min):
  [ ] Dishes washed / dishwasher loaded and run
  [ ] Counters wiped down
  [ ] Stovetop wiped (after last use)
  [ ] Sink cleaned (scrub with baking soda, rinse)
  [ ] Table/eating surfaces wiped
  [ ] Sweep floor (or quick vacuum of high-traffic area)

BATHROOMS (2-3 min each):
  [ ] Wipe counter and sink after morning routine
  [ ] Squeegee shower door/walls after last shower of the day
  [ ] Quick toilet wipe (outside surfaces, seat)
  [ ] Hang up towels

GENERAL (5-7 min):
  [ ] Make all beds (immediately upon rising)
  [ ] One load of laundry (start, switch, or fold/put away)
  [ ] 10-minute family tidy (everyone picks up their things)
  [ ] Sort and deal with mail
  [ ] Take out trash if full
  [ ] Quick sweep of entryway

MINDSET: Daily cleaning is about maintaining, not deep cleaning.
Good enough IS good enough. Consistency beats intensity.
```

## Speed Cleaning Techniques

### The Professional Speed Clean Method
```
SPEED CLEANING RULES (from professionals):
RULE 1: WORK TOP TO BOTTOM
  Dust falls. Start with ceiling fans, then shelves, then counters,
  then floors. Never clean floors first.
RULE 2: WORK LEFT TO RIGHT
  Pick a starting point and work around the room in one direction.
  You won't miss anything and won't backtrack.
RULE 3: WORK DRY TO WET
  Dust first, then spray and wipe. Dry dusting before wet cleaning
  prevents mud streaks.
RULE 4: LET PRODUCTS DO THE WORK
  Spray surfaces and move on. Come back to wipe after the product
  has had 2-5 minutes to dissolve grime. Don't scrub immediately.
RULE 5: CARRY YOUR SUPPLIES
  Every trip costs time.
RULE 6: TWO-CLOTH SYSTEM
  One damp cloth for wiping, one dry cloth for polishing/drying.
  Or: spray and wipe with microfiber (usually sufficient).
RULE 7: MINIMIZE TRIPS
  Take a trash bag with you. Collect all trash in one pass.
  Carry a "put away" basket for items that go in other rooms.
  Distribute items to their homes at the end, not mid-clean.
```

### Room Speed Clean Protocols
```
BATHROOM SPEED CLEAN (8-10 minutes):
0:00 - Spray toilet bowl cleaner inside bowl, let sit
0:30 - Spray all surfaces: counter, mirror, tub, fixtures
1:00 - Wipe mirror (dry microfiber or glass cloth)
1:30 - Wipe counter, faucet, and sink
2:30 - Wipe toilet exterior (top to bottom, don't skip base)
3:30 - Scrub toilet bowl with brush, flush
4:30 - Spray and wipe tub/shower (quick wipe, not deep scrub)
6:00 - Wipe fixtures and handles
7:00 - Sweep floor
8:00 - Damp mop floor
9:00 - Empty trash, replace towels
10:00 - Done!
KITCHEN SPEED CLEAN (12-15 minutes):
0:00 - Clear and wipe table/eating surfaces
1:00 - Load dishwasher with any dishes
3:00 - Spray counters, stove, appliance fronts
4:00 - Wipe counters (left to right around the kitchen)
6:00 - Wipe stovetop
7:00 - Wipe appliance fronts (microwave, fridge, dishwasher)
9:00 - Clean sink (scrub, rinse)
10:00 - Sweep floor
12:00 - Quick mop (or Swiffer wet)
14:00 - Take out trash if needed
15:00 - Done!
LIVING ROOM SPEED CLEAN (10-12 minutes):
0:00 - Pick up all items that don't belong (into basket)
2:00 - Fluff and arrange pillows/throws
3:00 - Quick dust: TV, tables, shelves (microfiber)
6:00 - Wipe glass surfaces (coffee table, TV screen)
8:00 - Vacuum (focus on high-traffic areas)
11:00 - Distribute basket items to proper rooms
12:00 - Done!
```

## Stain Removal Guide

### Quick Reference Stain Chart
```
STAIN REMOVAL GUIDE (act quickly -- fresh stains are easier):
GOLDEN RULES:
  1. Blot, never rub (rubbing spreads and sets stains)
  2. Work from outside of stain inward
  3. Cold water for protein stains (blood, milk, egg)
  4. Hot water for grease stains
  5. Test any treatment in an inconspicuous area first
  6. Treat before laundering -- dryer heat sets stains permanently
FABRIC STAINS:
BLOOD:
  Fresh: Cold water rinse immediately, then hydrogen peroxide, then wash cold
  Dried: Soak in cold salt water (1 tbsp salt per cup), then hydrogen peroxide
RED WINE:
  Immediately: Blot, cover with salt, let absorb, brush off
  Then: Club soda or white wine to dilute
  Launder: With oxygen bleach (OxiClean)
  Alternative: 1 part dish soap + 1 part hydrogen peroxide, apply, wait 30 min
COFFEE/TEA:
  Rinse with cold water from the back of the fabric
  Apply dish soap, gently work in, rinse
  If persistent: soak in oxygen bleach solution
GREASE/OIL:
  Sprinkle with baking soda or cornstarch, let sit 15 min to absorb
  Brush off, apply dish soap directly, work in, rinse hot
  Launder in hottest safe temperature for fabric
INK:
  Ballpoint: Rubbing alcohol on cotton ball, blot repeatedly
  Permanent marker: Rubbing alcohol, hand sanitizer, or hairspray
  Place paper towel behind stain to absorb ink transfer
GRASS:
  Apply rubbing alcohol or white vinegar directly
  Scrub gently with old toothbrush
  Launder with enzyme-based detergent
PET STAINS (urine):
  Blot up as much as possible
  Enzyme cleaner is essential (Nature's Miracle, Rocco & Roxie)
  Vinegar + baking soda for odor if enzyme cleaner isn't available
CARPET STAINS:
GENERAL CARPET STAIN PROCESS:
  1. Blot excess (spoons for solids, cloth for liquids)
  2. Apply club soda or carpet cleaner
  3. Blot from outside in
  4. Rinse with clean water, blot dry
  5. Place clean white cloth with heavy object on top to absorb remaining
  6. Vacuum once completely dry
HARD SURFACE STAINS:
HARD WATER DEPOSITS (white buildup):
  Soak paper towel in white vinegar, lay over deposit
  Let sit 30-60 minutes
  Scrub with baking soda paste for stubborn spots
  Rinse thoroughly
MOLD/MILDEW:
  Hydrogen peroxide (3%) in spray bottle
  Spray, wait 10 minutes, scrub, rinse
  Address moisture source to prevent recurrence
SOAP SCUM:
  Dish soap + vinegar spray (equal parts, warm vinegar)
  Spray, let sit 15 minutes, scrub with non-scratch pad
  Magic Eraser works well on stubborn scum
```

## Cleaning with Kids and Pets
```
CLEANING WITH CHILDREN IN THE HOME:
Age-appropriate cleaning tasks: (See Chore Organizer skill for full list)
TIPS:
  - Lower your standards appropriately (their clean is different from your clean)
  - Make it routine, not punishment
  - Clean together -- model the behavior
  - Use music to make it fun (cleaning dance party)
  - Acknowledge their effort specifically
PET-SPECIFIC CLEANING:
DAILY:
  [ ] Vacuum pet hair from main areas (rubber broom for hard floors)
  [ ] Wipe paws after outdoor walks
  [ ] Wash food and water bowls
WEEKLY:
  [ ] Wash pet bedding
  [ ] Vacuum furniture where pets sit (use pet hair attachment)
  [ ] Clean litter box area thoroughly (or daily scooping for litter)
  [ ] Mop hard floors (pet-safe cleaner)
PET HAIR REMOVAL TRICKS:
  - Rubber gloves on upholstery (dampened, rub in one direction)
  - Lint roller for clothing and furniture
  - Damp rubber broom for hard floors (hair clumps for easy pickup)
  - Dryer sheets on baseboards (reduces static that attracts hair)
  - Vacuum with HEPA filter (traps allergens)
  - Robot vacuum for daily maintenance (game changer for pet owners)
PET-SAFE CLEANING PRODUCTS:
  Safe: Vinegar, baking soda, hydrogen peroxide, dish soap
  When in doubt: rinse surfaces thoroughly after cleaning
```
