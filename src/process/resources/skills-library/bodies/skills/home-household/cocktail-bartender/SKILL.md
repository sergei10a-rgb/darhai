## Core Techniques

### Shaking

**When to shake:** Any cocktail containing citrus juice, cream, egg, or fruit.

```
1. Add ingredients to the small tin of a Boston shaker
2. Fill large tin 2/3 with ice
3. Seal firmly (tap the top with your palm)
4. Shake hard for 12-15 seconds (ice should rattle violently,
   tin should become painfully cold)
5. Break the seal, strain through a Hawthorne strainer

Dry Shaking: For egg white cocktails — shake 15 seconds
without ice first to emulsify, then add ice and shake again.
```

### Stirring

**When to stir:** Spirit-forward cocktails with no citrus, cream, or fruit (Old Fashioned, Manhattan, Martini, Negroni).

```
1. Add ingredients and ice to a mixing glass
2. Stir in a smooth, continuous motion for 20-40 seconds
   (spoon should glide along the inside wall)
3. Strain through a julep strainer into the serving glass
```

### Muddling

**When to muddle:** To extract flavor from fresh herbs, fruit, or sugar.

- For herbs (mint, basil): PRESS gently to bruise and release oils. Do not pulverize.
- For fruit (lime wedges, berries): Press firmly to extract juice.
- For sugar cubes: Press and twist to dissolve with bitters.

### Expressing Citrus Oils

Cut a coin-sized piece of citrus peel (no pith). Hold over the drink, colored side down, and pinch firmly -- you will see a fine mist of oils spray onto the surface. Optionally run the peel around the rim.

## Spirit Categories and Modifiers

### The Six Base Spirits

- **Vodka:** Neutral, clean, versatile carrier for other flavors
- **Gin:** Juniper-forward with botanical complexity (London Dry is classic)
- **Rum:** White for light cocktails; aged for depth; dark for richness
- **Tequila:** Blanco (bright, clean) for cocktails; Reposado (balanced) for sipping cocktails
- **Whiskey:** Bourbon (sweet, corn), Rye (spicy, herbal), Scotch (smoky/fruity), Irish (smooth)
- **Brandy/Cognac:** Distilled from wine -- rich, fruity, warming

### Essential Modifiers

| Category | Examples | Used In |
|---|---|---|
| Sweet vermouth | Carpano Antica, Dolin Rouge | Manhattan, Negroni |
| Dry vermouth | Dolin Dry, Noilly Prat | Martini |
| Orange liqueur | Cointreau, Grand Marnier | Margarita, Sidecar |
| Bitter liqueur | Campari, Aperol | Negroni, Spritz |
| Coffee liqueur | Kahlua, Mr. Black | Espresso Martini |

## Ice and Garnishes

### Ice Types

| Type | Best For |
|---|---|
| Standard cubes | Shaking, building tall drinks |
| Large cubes (2 inch) | Rocks drinks -- slow melting, minimal dilution |
| Crushed ice | Juleps, Tiki drinks, swizzles |
| Clear ice | Stirred drinks, presentation (use directional freezing in a cooler) |

**Ice rules:** Always use fresh, clean-tasting ice. Fill the shaker or glass fully -- more ice means faster chilling and less dilution. Never reuse shaking/stirring ice. Chill glassware in the freezer before serving.

### Essential Garnishes

- **Citrus wheel/wedge:** Slice crosswise 1/4 inch; slit to rim
- **Citrus twist:** Y-peeler strip of zest, twist over drink
- **Herb sprig:** Slap between palms to release oils before placing
- **Cherry:** Luxardo maraschino (not bright red sundae cherries)
- **Olive:** Quality cocktail olives for Martinis

## Batching for Parties

### The Batching Formula

```
1. Determine the recipe for one drink
2. Multiply by the number of servings
3. Combine all ingredients EXCEPT carbonated elements
4. Add 15-20% water to account for dilution (commonly missed step)
5. Refrigerate (ideally overnight)
6. Serve over fresh ice; add carbonation per glass at serving time

Example: Batched Margaritas for 12
  24 oz tequila, 12 oz lime juice, 9 oz Cointreau
  Add: ~5 oz water (15% of 45 oz total)
  Combine, refrigerate, serve over ice with salt rim
```

### Punch Format

```
Classic Punch Ratio (per serving, then scale):
  2 parts spirit / 1 part citrus / 1 part sweet / 3 parts weak (water, tea, juice, soda)
Combine spirits, citrus, and sweet. Refrigerate. Add weak at party time.
Serve over a large block of ice in a punch bowl.
```

## Non-Alcoholic Alternatives

The same balance principles apply: you need body, acidity, sweetness, and dilution.

**Spirit substitutes:** Seedlip (botanical distillate), Lyre's (non-alcoholic spirit replicas), strong tea (provides tannin and body), shrubs (drinking vinegars for complexity).

**Quick zero-proof recipes:**
- **Virgin Margarita:** 2 oz OJ, 1 oz lime, 0.5 oz agave, pinch of salt -- shake, strain over ice
- **Nojito:** Muddle mint with lime and simple syrup, add ice, top with soda and splash of ginger ale
- **Espresso Tonic:** 4 oz tonic over ice, slowly pour 1 oz espresso over the back of a spoon for layering

## Guiding Principles

1. **Fresh juice changes everything.** Never use bottled lemon or lime juice.
2. **Measure precisely.** Use a jigger every time. Balance depends on accuracy.
3. **Quality ice matters.** Fresh, abundant ice -- a well-iced drink is colder and paradoxically less diluted.
4. **Taste and adjust.** Every lime is different, every palate is unique.
5. **Chill your glassware.** A cold glass keeps a drink cold.
6. **Simplicity is sophistication.** A perfect three-ingredient cocktail beats a mediocre seven-ingredient one.
7. **Always offer a non-alcoholic option.** Great hospitality means everyone feels welcome.
