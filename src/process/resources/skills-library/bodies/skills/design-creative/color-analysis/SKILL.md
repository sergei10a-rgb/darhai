# Personal Color Analysis
> **NOTE:** Color analysis is a guide, not a rigid rulebook. These frameworks help you discover colors that naturally enhance your appearance, but personal preference and confidence matter most. Wear what makes you feel great.
## When to Use

**Use this skill when:**
- User asks about color analysis
- User needs guidance on color analysis topics
- User wants a structured approach to color analysis

**Do NOT use when:**
- Request requires professional consultation beyond educational guidance

## Phase 1: Understanding Undertones

### The Undertone System
```
Every person's skin has an UNDERTONE beneath the surface color.
Undertone does NOT change with tanning, aging, or seasons.
Surface color (light to dark) is different from undertone (warm, cool, neutral).

THREE UNDERTONE CATEGORIES:

WARM UNDERTONE:
- Veins appear greenish
- Gold jewelry is more flattering than silver
- Skin has yellow, golden, or peachy hue
- Tans easily, rarely burns severely
- White clothing can look harsh; cream/ivory is better
- Earth tones and warm colors look natural

COOL UNDERTONE:
- Veins appear blue or purple
- Silver jewelry is more flattering than gold
- Skin has pink, red, or bluish hue
- Burns easily, tans slowly
- Pure white looks crisp and clean
- Jewel tones and cool colors look natural

NEUTRAL UNDERTONE:
- Veins appear blue-green (mix of both)
- Both gold and silver jewelry look good
- Skin is a balanced mix of warm and cool
- Can wear both warm and cool colors
- Olive skin often falls in this category
- Most versatile for color choices
```

### Undertone Identification Tests
```
TEST 1: THE VEIN TEST
Look at the veins on the inside of your wrist in natural daylight:
- Blue/Purple veins = Cool undertone
- Green veins = Warm undertone
- Both blue and green = Neutral undertone

TEST 2: THE JEWELRY TEST
Hold silver jewelry next to your face, then gold:
- Silver looks better = Cool
- Gold looks better = Warm
- Both look equally good = Neutral

TEST 3: THE WHITE PAPER TEST
Hold a plain white sheet of paper next to your bare face:
- Skin looks yellowish/golden against white = Warm
- Skin looks pinkish/rosy against white = Cool
- Skin looks grayish or balanced = Neutral

TEST 4: THE SUN TEST
How does your skin react to sun exposure?
- Burns easily, rarely tans = Likely cool
- Tans easily, golden color = Likely warm
- Burns then tans = Could be either

TEST 5: THE FABRIC DRAPING TEST (Most Accurate)
Hold different colored fabrics near your face in natural light:
- If warm colors (orange, rust, olive) make you glow = Warm
- If cool colors (fuchsia, royal blue, emerald) make you glow = Cool
- If both work well = Neutral

Look for: Skin appears healthier, eyes brighter, features more defined
vs: Skin looks washed out, shadows under eyes, features blurred
```

## Phase 2: Seasonal Color Analysis

### The Four Seasons System
```
The seasonal system categorizes people into four groups based on:
1. UNDERTONE (Warm or Cool)
2. VALUE (Light or Deep/Dark)
3. CHROMA (Muted/Soft or Bright/Clear)

        | WARM           | COOL
--------|----------------|----------------
LIGHT   | SPRING         | SUMMER
DEEP    | AUTUMN         | WINTER

Each season has sub-seasons for more precision (12-season system).
```

### SPRING (Warm + Light + Bright)
```
CHARACTERISTICS:
- Warm undertone with light or clear quality
- Hair: Golden blonde, strawberry blonde, light brown with warm highlights
- Eyes: Blue, green, light hazel, warm brown
- Skin: Light with warm golden or peachy tone, may freckle
- Overall impression: Fresh, warm, clear, youthful

BEST COLORS:
Neutrals: Warm white (ivory), camel, light warm gray, warm beige
Reds: Coral, salmon, warm red (tomato red), peach
Blues: Periwinkle, turquoise, aqua, clear light blue
Greens: Lime, mint, spring green, bright leaf green
Yellows: Bright yellow, daffodil, golden yellow
Pinks: Coral pink, peach, warm pink, salmon
Purples: Light warm purple, orchid, violet
Earth tones: Golden tan, warm sand, light caramel

AVOID:
- Black (too harsh, use warm dark brown or navy instead)
- Cool gray
- Burgundy (too cool and dark)
- Ice blue or icy pastels
- Muted or dusty colors (they dull your brightness)

COLOR PALETTE (Hex codes for reference):
Coral: #FF6F61 | Peach: #FFDAB9 | Aqua: #7FFFD4
Warm Red: #E14D2A | Periwinkle: #8E82FE | Lime: #BFFF00
Ivory: #FFFFF0 | Camel: #C19A6B | Golden Yellow: #FFD700
```

### SUMMER (Cool + Light + Muted)
```
CHARACTERISTICS:
- Cool undertone with light, soft, muted quality
- Hair: Ash blonde, light ash brown, mousy brown, gray
- Eyes: Soft blue, gray-blue, light green, soft hazel, rose-brown
- Skin: Light with pink, rose, or neutral undertone, can look porcelain
- Overall impression: Soft, elegant, cool, gentle

BEST COLORS:
Neutrals: Soft white, rose-brown, taupe, cool gray, slate blue
Reds: Rose, raspberry, soft burgundy, muted red
Blues: Powder blue, sky blue, slate blue, denim blue, soft navy
Greens: Sage, seafoam, muted teal, soft mint
Yellows: Soft lemon (sparingly), light primrose
Pinks: Rose, mauve, dusty pink, lavender-pink
Purples: Lavender, plum, soft purple, mauve
Earth tones: Taupe, rose-brown, soft cocoa

AVOID:
- Bright orange or neon colors (too intense)
- Warm yellow-greens
- Black (too stark, use charcoal or navy)
- Rust or warm earth tones
- Very bright, saturated colors

COLOR PALETTE:

### AUTUMN (Warm + Deep + Muted)
```
CHARACTERISTICS:
- Warm undertone with deep, rich, earthy quality
- Hair: Auburn, red, dark brown with warm tones, warm chestnut, copper
- Eyes: Dark brown, amber, olive green, warm hazel, dark hazel
- Skin: Warm with golden, olive, or bronze tones, tans well
- Overall impression: Rich, warm, earthy, deep

BEST COLORS:
Neutrals: Warm brown, chocolate, olive, camel, dark warm beige, off-white

Pinks: Salmon, warm rose, peach (deeper shades)
Purples: Eggplant, aubergine, warm plum

AVOID:
- Icy pastels (wash you out)
- Bright cool pink or fuchsia
- Royal blue (too cool and bright)
- Cool gray
- Black (use dark chocolate, charcoal-brown, or dark olive)
- Neon or electric colors

### WINTER (Cool + Deep + Bright)
```
CHARACTERISTICS:
- Cool undertone with deep, high-contrast, vivid quality
- Hair: Black, very dark brown, cool dark brown, silver/white
- Eyes: Dark brown, black-brown, icy blue, vivid green, cool hazel
- Skin: Can range from very fair (porcelain) to very deep, always cool undertone
- Overall impression: Dramatic, striking, high-contrast, bold

BEST COLORS:
Neutrals: True black, pure white, charcoal, navy, icy gray
Reds: True red, cherry, burgundy, cranberry, wine

Jewel tones: All jewel tones work beautifully

AVOID:
- Warm beige or camel (too warm and muted)
- Orange (too warm)
- Warm golden tones
- Muted, dusty colors (they dim your natural contrast)
- Earth tones (too warm for your coloring)
- Warm browns

## Phase 3: The 12-Season Refined System

### Sub-Seasons Overview
```
Each main season has 3 sub-seasons:

SPRING:
- Light Spring: Lightest, most delicate warm coloring
- True/Warm Spring: Classic warm, golden coloring
- Bright/Clear Spring: High contrast with warm undertone

SUMMER:
- Light Summer: Lightest, most delicate cool coloring
- True/Cool Summer: Classic cool, muted coloring
- Soft Summer: Most muted, blended cool-neutral coloring

AUTUMN:
- Soft Autumn: Lightest, most muted warm coloring
- True/Warm Autumn: Classic warm, rich coloring
- Deep/Dark Autumn: Deepest, richest warm coloring

WINTER:
- Deep/Dark Winter: Deepest, high-contrast cool coloring
- True/Cool Winter: Classic cool, vivid coloring
- Bright/Clear Winter: Most vivid, high-contrast cool coloring

FINDING YOUR SUB-SEASON:
After identifying your main season, ask:
1. Am I lighter or deeper than typical for this season? (Light or Deep)
2. Am I more muted or more bright? (Soft/True or Clear/Bright)
3. Am I firmly in this season or do I share traits with an adjacent season?
```

## Phase 4: Hair Color Considerations

### Hair Color by Season
```
SPRING (Warm + Light):
Best hair colors: Golden blonde, honey, strawberry blonde, light golden brown
Highlights: Warm gold, caramel, honey
Avoid: Ash tones, cool platinum, blue-black

SUMMER (Cool + Light):
Best hair colors: Ash blonde, cool brown, mushroom brown, platinum
Highlights: Cool blonde, ash, icy
Avoid: Warm golden tones, copper, auburn

AUTUMN (Warm + Deep):
Best hair colors: Auburn, copper, warm chocolate, chestnut, warm dark brown
Highlights: Copper, golden, caramel
Avoid: Ash tones, platinum, cool black

WINTER (Cool + Deep):
Best hair colors: Dark espresso, blue-black, cool dark brown, platinum (extreme)
Highlights: Cool tones only if highlighting, icy
Avoid: Warm golden highlights, copper, brassy tones

GOING GRAY/SILVER:
- Cool seasons: Silver and gray are naturally flattering
- Warm seasons: Consider a warm toner or embrace the transition
- Gray hair shifts most people slightly toward the Summer or Winter palette
- Adjust makeup and clothing colors as hair transitions
```

## Phase 5: Makeup Color Matching

### Makeup by Season
```
SPRING MAKEUP:
Foundation: Warm, golden undertone
Blush: Peach, coral, warm pink
Lipstick: Coral, peach, warm nude, salmon
Eye shadow: Warm taupe, peach, soft gold, warm bronze
Eyeliner: Warm brown (softer than black)
Mascara: Brown or brown-black

SUMMER MAKEUP:
Foundation: Cool pink or neutral undertone
Blush: Rose, soft pink, mauve
Lipstick: Rose, mauve, berry, cool nude, plum
Eye shadow: Cool taupe, mauve, slate, soft plum
Eyeliner: Cool brown, gray, soft charcoal
Mascara: Brown-black or black

AUTUMN MAKEUP:
Foundation: Warm golden or olive undertone
Blush: Brick, terracotta, warm bronze, deep peach
Lipstick: Brick red, warm nude, copper, spice
Eye shadow: Warm brown, bronze, olive, copper, gold
Eyeliner: Dark brown, olive, bronze
Mascara: Dark brown or brown-black

WINTER MAKEUP:
Foundation: Cool or neutral undertone
Blush: Berry, cool pink, fuchsia (on apples of cheeks)
Lipstick: True red, berry, wine, fuchsia, cool nude
Eye shadow: Cool gray, silver, charcoal, plum, deep jewel tones
Eyeliner: Black (classic and flattering)
Mascara: Black
```

### Universal Flattering Colors (Work for Everyone)
```
UNIVERSALLY FLATTERING LIPSTICK SHADES:
- Berry (adjusts to look cool or warm on different skin tones)
- Rosy mauve (soft, neutral)
- "Your lips but better" shade (one shade deeper than natural lip)
- Soft red (not too blue, not too orange)

UNIVERSALLY FLATTERING CLOTHING:
- Teal (balanced warm-cool)
- Soft red (not too warm, not too cool)
- Blush pink (soft enough for most people)
- True medium gray (neutral)
- Denim blue (universally wearable)
```

WARDROBE COLOR PLANNING TEMPLATE:

Your Season: _______________

1. _______________ (darkest neutral)
2. _______________ (medium neutral)
3. _______________ (light neutral)

ACCENT COLORS (30% of wardrobe):
1. _______________ (best jewel/rich tone)
2. _______________ (secondary accent)
3. _______________ (pop of personality)

METALLICS (10% - accessories, jewelry):
Best metal: Gold / Silver / Rose Gold
Best metallic accents: _______________

PATTERN GUIDANCE:
Choose patterns that combine your best colors:
- Stripes in your neutrals + one accent
- Florals with your palette's warm or cool base
- Plaids in your season's color family
- Prints that use 2-3 of your best colors
```

## Phase 7: Color Psychology

### What Colors Communicate
```
RED: Power, energy, passion, urgency
- Wear for: Job interviews (power tie), first dates, presentations
- Effect: Increases heart rate in observers, commands attention
- Use sparingly: Too much can be aggressive or overwhelming

BLUE: Trust, calm, intelligence, reliability
- Wear for: Business meetings, first days, professional settings
- Effect: Most universally liked color, builds trust
- Navy is the safest power color for both genders

BLACK: Sophistication, authority, elegance, mystery
- Wear for: Formal events, evening, creative settings, minimalist look
- Effect: Slimming, universally polished
- Risk: Can read as unapproachable or somber

WHITE: Purity, freshness, cleanliness, simplicity
- Wear for: Summer, fresh starts, professional settings
- Effect: Clean, modern, crisp
- Risk: Shows every imperfection (stains, wrinkles)

GREEN: Growth, balance, nature, prosperity
- Wear for: Casual settings, creative meetings, wellness contexts
- Effect: Calming, refreshing
- Easiest color on the eyes

YELLOW: Optimism, happiness, creativity, energy
- Wear for: Creative settings, casual occasions, spring/summer
- Effect: Attention-getting, cheerful
- Risk: Can overwhelm, use as an accent

PURPLE: Creativity, luxury, wisdom, royalty
- Wear for: Creative settings, evening events, standing out
- Effect: Distinctive, elegant
- Deep purple = regal; light lavender = soft and romantic

GRAY: Neutrality, professionalism, sophistication, balance
- Wear for: Business, versatile everyday, pairing with bold accents
- Effect: Professional without being severe
- Light gray is casual; charcoal is professional

BROWN: Stability, warmth, reliability, earthiness
- Wear for: Casual professional, outdoor events, autumn
- Effect: Approachable, grounded
- Pairs naturally with most colors
```
