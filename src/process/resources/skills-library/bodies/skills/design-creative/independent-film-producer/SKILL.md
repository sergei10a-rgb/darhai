## Financing

```
INDIE FILM FINANCING SOURCES:

EQUITY INVESTMENT:
- Private investors (high-net-worth individuals)
- Investment entities (production companies, funds)
- Structure: LLC with investor shares, revenue waterfall
- Investors typically want recoupment before profit share
- Be transparent about risk (most indie films lose money)

GRANTS:
- Sundance Labs, IFP, Film Independent
- State film commissions (production incentives)
- Arts councils and cultural foundations
- NEA, NEH for documentary and arts-focused work
- Application-intensive but non-dilutive

TAX INCENTIVES:
- State film tax credits (varies by state, 15-30% of spend)
- International co-production incentives
- Can be sold or transferred to offset budget
- Research current incentives (they change frequently)

PRE-SALES:
- Sell distribution rights to territories before production
- Requires bankable talent or strong concept
- Sales agent helps negotiate and package
- Minimum guarantees fund production

CROWDFUNDING:
- Kickstarter, Indiegogo, Seed&Spark
- Best for: projects with built-in communities
- Marketing intensive (plan 2-3 months of active campaigning)
- Realistic for $10K-$100K range

GAP FINANCING:
- Loans against unsold territory rights
- Bridge between pre-sales and production start
- Higher risk, higher interest
- Requires strong sales estimates
```

## Production Management

```
BUDGET CATEGORIES:
Above the Line (ATL): Writer, director, producers, principal cast
Below the Line (BTL): Crew, equipment, locations, post-production
Other: Insurance, legal, contingency, deliverables

BUDGET ALLOCATION (typical micro/low budget):
Above the Line:    15-20%
Production:        40-50%
Post-Production:   15-20%
Marketing/Delivery: 5-10%
Contingency:       10% (non-negotiable, always include this)

SCHEDULING:
- Schedule based on actor availability and location access
- Shoot out of continuity to maximize location use
- Group all scenes at each location together
- Plan for weather contingencies on exterior days
- Build in rest days (crew fatigue causes mistakes and injuries)
- Typical indie: 15-25 shooting days for a feature

KEY CREW PRIORITIES (hire the best you can afford):
1. Director of Photography (visual quality of the film)
2. Sound mixer (bad sound cannot be fixed in post)
3. 1st Assistant Director (keeps production on schedule)
4. Line Producer/UPM (manages the money)
5. Production Designer (visual storytelling)
```

## Festival Strategy

```
FESTIVAL TIERS:
Tier 1 (premiere): Sundance, Cannes, Venice, Toronto, Berlin, SXSW
  - Launch pad for distribution deals
  - World premiere required
  - Extremely competitive (1-2% acceptance)

Tier 2 (major): Tribeca, Telluride, Locarno, Rotterdam, New York
  - Strong industry presence
  - Can lead to distribution
  - North American or international premiere required

Tier 3 (strong regional/genre): Fantastic Fest, Outfest, DOC NYC, SIFF
  - Great audience exposure
  - Genre-specific networking
  - Less restrictive premiere requirements

SUBMISSION STRATEGY:
- Start with Tier 1 (you have nothing to lose)
- Submit to 15-25 festivals total across tiers
- Budget $2,000-5,000 for submission fees
- Use early bird deadlines (cheaper and shows organization)
- Have a festival publicist (or learn to pitch your film)

FESTIVAL TIMELINE:
Completion -> submit to top festivals -> respond to invitations ->
prepare for premiere -> attend festival -> take meetings ->
continue festival circuit -> negotiate distribution
```

## Distribution

```
DISTRIBUTION OPTIONS:
Type              | Revenue Potential | Audience Reach | Control
------------------+-------------------+----------------+---------
Theatrical (ltd)  | Low-Medium        | Targeted       | Low
Streaming deal    | Medium-High       | High           | Low
Self-distribution | Variable          | Variable       | High
Hybrid            | Medium            | Moderate       | Moderate
TVOD/EST          | Low-Medium        | Moderate       | Moderate

DISTRIBUTOR EVALUATION:
- What is their track record with similar films?
- What is the MG (minimum guarantee)?
- What are the expenses they will deduct?
- What territories do they cover?
- What is their marketing plan and budget?
- How transparent is their reporting?
- What rights are you granting and for how long?

SELF-DISTRIBUTION TOOLS:
- Vimeo OTT / Gumroad / own website for direct sales
- Distributor of Record services for streaming platforms
- Social media marketing and email lists
- Event screenings and community partnerships

DELIVERABLES CHECKLIST:
- DCP (Digital Cinema Package) for theatrical
- ProRes master (various specs per platform)
- M&E track (music and effects, no dialogue - for international)
- Closed captions and subtitles
- Key art (poster) in multiple sizes
- Trailer
- Press kit (stills, synopsis, bios, production notes)
- Chain of title documents
- E&O insurance certificate
```

## Marketing an Independent Film

```
MARKETING TIMELINE:

PRE-PRODUCTION:
- Build social media presence for the project
- Behind-the-scenes content strategy
- Press kit started (evolve through production)
- Media contacts list building

PRODUCTION:
- Daily or weekly BTS content (photos, short videos)
- Grow following with authentic production content
- Stills photographer on set (essential, not optional)
- Unit publicist for larger productions

POST-PRODUCTION:
- Teaser trailer (3-6 months before premiere)
- Full trailer (6-8 weeks before release)
- Press screenings (4-6 weeks before)
- Review embargoes and premieres
- Festival publicity campaign

RELEASE:
- Press tour / interviews
- Social media campaign
- Email list activation
- Screenings and events
- Advertising (targeted, within budget)

MARKETING BUDGET ALLOCATION:
Micro-budget: $1,000-5,000 (social media, festival screenings)
Low budget: $5,000-25,000 (publicist, basic ad spend, events)
Mid budget: $25,000-100,000 (publicist, P&A, theatrical)
```

## Legal and Business Affairs

```
ESSENTIAL DOCUMENTS:

CHAIN OF TITLE:
- Proves you have the right to make and sell this film
- Starts with: option/purchase agreement for underlying material
  (script, book, life rights)
- Includes: writer agreements, director agreement, all contributor agreements
- Distributors and insurers will not touch your film without clean chain of title

KEY CONTRACTS:
- Option/Purchase Agreement (for script or source material)
- Writer Agreement (WGA or non-WGA)
- Director Agreement (DGA or non-DGA)
- Actor Agreements (SAG-AFTRA or non-union)
- Crew Deal Memos (for each department head)
- Location Agreements (permission to film)
- Music Licenses (sync and master for any music used)
- Distribution Agreement (when you sell the film)

INSURANCE (required):
- General liability
- Workers compensation
- Equipment/props coverage
- Errors and omissions (E&O) - required for distribution
- Vehicle insurance (if driving production vehicles)

LLC STRUCTURE:
- Create a single-purpose LLC for each film
- Protects personal assets and other projects
- Clean entity for investment and revenue
- Standard structure: LLC with managing member (you) and investor members
```
