## Revenue Streams

```
MODERN MUSIC REVENUE MAP:

RECORDED MUSIC:
- Streaming royalties (Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music, etc.)
- Download sales (declining but still exists)
- Physical sales (vinyl resurgence, CDs, special editions)
- Licensing fees (sync, samples, covers)

LIVE PERFORMANCE:
- Ticket sales / guarantees
- Merchandise at shows
- Meet and greet / VIP packages
- Festival fees

PUBLISHING / SONGWRITING:
- Mechanical royalties (reproduction of songs)
- Performance royalties (radio, live, streaming)
- Sync licensing (TV, film, ads, games)
- Print royalties (sheet music)

MERCHANDISE:
- Direct-to-fan (website, shows)
- Third-party retail
- Branded partnerships

OTHER:
- Fan subscriptions (Patreon, Bandcamp, YouTube memberships)
- Teaching (lessons, masterclasses, courses)
- Brand partnerships and endorsements
- Session work and production for others
- Content creator income (YouTube, TikTok)
```

```
CHOOSING A DISTRIBUTOR:
- How much control do you want to retain?
- Do you need advance funding?
- What analytics and tools do they provide?
- What is their commission structure?
- Do they offer playlist pitching or marketing support?
- What is their payment schedule? (Monthly, quarterly)

RELEASE STRATEGY:
- Singles every 4-6 weeks builds algorithmic momentum
- Album releases still work for established audiences
- Pre-save campaigns boost release-day performance
- Submit to streaming editorial playlists 4 weeks early
- Coordinate social content around release dates
```

## Royalties Explained

```
STREAMING ROYALTIES (per stream, approximate):
Platform        | Per Stream (avg)  | Notes
----------------+-------------------+---------------------------
Spotify         | $0.003-0.005      | Pro-rata model
Apple Music     | $0.007-0.010      | Higher per stream
YouTube Music   | $0.002-0.004      | Ad-supported + premium
Amazon Music    | $0.004-0.008      | Varies by tier
Tidal           | $0.008-0.012      | Highest per stream

Note: Per-stream rates vary by country, subscription type, and total streams.

WHO GETS PAID FROM A STREAM:
1. Platform takes ~30%
2. Remaining 70% goes to rights holders:
   - Master recording owner (label or artist): ~80% of remainder
   - Publishers and songwriters: ~20% of remainder
   - Split further among all credited writers and publishers

ROYALTY TYPES:
Mechanical: Paid for reproduction of a song (streaming, downloads, CDs)
Performance: Paid for public performance (radio, live, streaming, TV)
Sync: One-time fee for use in visual media (negotiated per use)

COLLECTING ROYALTIES:
Register with ALL relevant organizations:
- Performance Rights Organization (ASCAP, BMI, or SESAC in US)
- Mechanical rights (Harry Fox Agency, or your publisher)
- SoundExchange (digital performance royalties for sound recordings)
- Your distributor (streaming royalties for masters)
- International collection societies if you have global audience
```

## Sync Licensing

```
SYNC LICENSING: Placing music in TV, film, ads, games, and online content.

WHY SYNC MATTERS:
- Lump sum payment (often $1,000-$100,000+ depending on placement)
- Exposure to new audiences
- Credibility boost
- Both master AND publishing rights are licensed (two payments)

GETTING SYNC PLACEMENTS:
1. Own your masters and publishing (or have clear split agreements)
2. Create instrumental versions of all songs
3. Register with sync licensing platforms (Musicbed, Artlist, Songtradr)
4. Work with a sync agent or music supervisor relationships
5. Create music that fits common sync needs:
   - Uplifting, positive energy (most in-demand)
   - Emotional, cinematic (film and drama)
   - Edgy, modern production (ads targeting young demographics)
6. Ensure all samples are cleared and all contributors are credited

SYNC DEAL STRUCTURE:
- Master use fee: paid to the owner of the recording
- Sync fee: paid to the owner of the composition (publishing)
- Term: how long the license lasts
- Territory: where it can be used
- Media: what types of media (TV, film, online, all)
- Exclusivity: exclusive use (more expensive) vs non-exclusive
```

## Building a Sustainable Career

```
CAREER PHASES:

PHASE 1: FOUNDATION (years 1-3)
- Create and release music consistently
- Build a small, engaged audience (1,000 true fans concept)
- Play live shows locally and regionally
- Build your email list (own your audience, not just social followers)
- Learn the business basics (royalties, rights, contracts)

PHASE 2: GROWTH (years 3-5)
- Professionalize (team up with manager, booking agent)
- Expand touring circuit (regional to national)
- Develop multiple revenue streams
- Build industry relationships (other artists, labels, publishers)
- Invest in marketing and audience development

PHASE 3: SUSTAINABILITY (years 5+)
- Diversify income across 4+ revenue streams
- Build assets (catalog value grows over time)
- Develop passive income (sync, catalog streaming, merch lines)
- Mentor and collaborate (expands network and relevance)
- Plan for longevity (save, invest, build beyond performing)

THE 1,000 TRUE FANS MODEL:
If 1,000 fans each spend $100/year on you:
  = $100,000/year in direct revenue
  Revenue sources: merch, tickets, music, subscriptions, special editions
  This is achievable and sustainable without mainstream fame.
```

## Touring Economics

```
TOUR FINANCIAL MODEL:

REVENUE:
- Ticket sales (guarantee vs door deal vs percentage)
- Merchandise sales (typically $5-15 per attendee)
- VIP/meet-and-greet packages
- Sponsorship (for larger tours)

EXPENSES:
- Transportation (van, bus, flights)
- Accommodation (hotels, Airbnb, or sleeping in the van)
- Crew costs (sound engineer, tour manager, techs)
- Per diems (food allowance per person per day)
- Backline rental (equipment you do not carry)
- Insurance (vehicle, gear, liability)
- Agent commission (10-15% of gross performance fee)
- Manager commission (15-20% of gross income)

BREAK-EVEN ANALYSIS:
At the club level (100-500 capacity):
  Guarantee: $200-2,000 per night
  Expenses: $150-500 per day (bare minimum, driving yourself)
  Merch: $200-1,000 per night (if you hustle)
  Reality: Most emerging artists lose money or break even touring
  Strategy: Tour to build audience, sell merch, and create content

At the theater level (500-2,000 capacity):
  Guarantee or split: $2,000-15,000 per night
  Expenses: $500-2,000 per day (small crew, hotel rooms)
  Merch: $1,000-5,000 per night
  Reality: Sustainable touring income possible at this level

ROUTING EFFICIENCY:
- Minimize driving distance between shows (map it out)
- Avoid long hauls for single shows (cluster dates in regions)
- Day-off cities should be cheap (not NYC or LA)
- Build in rest days every 4-5 shows
```

## Deal Structures

```
LABEL DEAL TYPES:

TRADITIONAL RECORD DEAL:
- Label funds recording, marketing, and distribution
- Label owns masters
- Artist receives 15-25% of revenue (after recoupment)
- Advances recouped from artist share
- Multi-album commitment typical

DISTRIBUTION DEAL:
- Artist funds recording and marketing
- Label/distributor handles distribution only
- Artist retains ownership of masters
- Distribution fee: 15-30% of revenue
- More common in modern industry

360 DEAL:
- Label participates in ALL revenue streams (merch, touring, etc.)
- Higher advances available
- Less artist control and independence
- More common with major labels

LICENSE DEAL:
- Artist delivers finished masters
- Label licenses for a defined period (3-7 years)
- Masters revert to artist after term
- Good balance of support and ownership

GENERAL ADVICE:
- Retain ownership of your masters if at all possible
- Understand recoupment before signing anything
- Have an entertainment lawyer review every deal
- Short terms are better than long terms
- Revenue share is negotiable - everything is negotiable
```
