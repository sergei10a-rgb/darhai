## CEFR-Aligned Study Path

### A1 - Beginner (0-3 months)

```
A1 CORE OBJECTIVES
====================
Grammar:
  - Present tense of regular -AR, -ER, -IR verbs
  - Ser vs. Estar (identity/characteristics vs. state/location)
  - Gender and number agreement (el/la/los/las)
  - Articles: definite and indefinite
  - Basic question words: que, donde, cuando, como, por que, quien
  - Hay (there is/there are)
  - Possessive adjectives (mi, tu, su, nuestro, vuestro)
  - Common irregular verbs: ir, tener, hacer, poder, querer

Vocabulary targets (500-800 words):
  - Greetings and farewells
  - Numbers 0-100
  - Days, months, seasons
  - Family members
  - Colors, food, clothing
  - Common adjectives (grande, pequeno, bueno, malo)
  - Directions and locations

Speaking milestones:
  - Introduce yourself and ask basic personal questions
  - Order food at a restaurant
  - Ask for and give directions
  - Describe your daily routine
```

### A2 - Elementary (3-6 months)

```
A2 CORE OBJECTIVES
====================
Grammar:
  - Preterite tense (completed past actions)
  - Imperfect tense (ongoing/habitual past)
  - Reflexive verbs (levantarse, ducharse, acostarse)
  - Direct and indirect object pronouns (lo, la, le, les)
  - Comparatives and superlatives (mas...que, el mas...)
  - Ir + a + infinitive (near future)
  - Gustar and similar verbs (encantar, interesar, molestar)

Vocabulary targets (1,500-2,000 words):
  - Travel and transportation
  - Shopping and money
  - Health and body parts
  - Weather expressions
  - Hobbies and free time
  - Work and professions

Speaking milestones:
  - Tell a story about a past experience
  - Make plans for the weekend
  - Describe symptoms to a doctor
  - Handle common travel situations
```

### B1 - Intermediate (6-12 months)

```
B1 CORE OBJECTIVES
====================
Grammar:
  - Present subjunctive (wishes, doubts, recommendations)
  - Conditional tense (would + verb)
  - Future tense (both ir + a and synthetic future)
  - Por vs. Para (common source of confusion)
  - Relative pronouns (que, quien, donde, lo que)
  - Perfect tenses (he comido, habia comido)
  - Imperative mood (commands: formal and informal)

Vocabulary targets (3,000-4,000 words):
  - Abstract concepts (freedom, justice, environment)
  - Academic and workplace vocabulary
  - Idiomatic expressions (20-30 common ones)
  - Connectors (sin embargo, ademas, por lo tanto)

Speaking milestones:
  - Express and defend opinions
  - Discuss current events
  - Handle unexpected situations while traveling
  - Participate in workplace discussions
```

### B2 - Upper Intermediate (12-18 months)

```
B2 CORE OBJECTIVES
====================
Grammar:
  - Subjunctive in all tenses (imperfect, pluperfect)
  - Si clauses (conditionals: real and unreal)
  - Passive voice and impersonal se
  - Advanced pronoun combinations (se lo dije)
  - Discourse markers and advanced connectors
  - Nuanced use of ser/estar with adjective changes

Vocabulary targets (5,000-6,000 words):
  - Field-specific terminology
  - Register variation (formal vs. colloquial)
  - Proverbs and cultural expressions
  - Regional vocabulary differences

Speaking milestones:
  - Debate abstract topics fluently
  - Understand and use humor and sarcasm
  - Present ideas in professional settings
  - Read literature and news without a dictionary
```

## Spanish Pronunciation Guide

### Vowel System

```
SPANISH VOWELS (5 pure vowels - simpler than English)
======================================================
a  /a/   - Open, like "father" but shorter
           Example: casa (house), hablar (to speak)

e  /e/   - Mid, like "pet" but without the glide
           Example: mesa (table), comer (to eat)

i  /i/   - Like "see" but shorter
           Example: libro (book), vivir (to live)

o  /o/   - Rounded, like "go" but without the glide
           Example: todo (all), poder (to be able)

u  /u/   - Like "food" but shorter
           Example: luz (light), mucho (much)

KEY PRINCIPLE: Spanish vowels are SHORT and PURE.
English speakers tend to add glides (say "oh" instead of "o").
Practice keeping vowels crisp and constant.
```

### Consonant Challenges

```
DIFFICULT CONSONANTS FOR ENGLISH SPEAKERS
===========================================
R (single):  Tongue tap against the ridge behind upper teeth
             Like the "tt" in American English "butter"
             Example: pero (but), caro (expensive)

RR (rolled):  Multiple rapid tongue taps (alveolar trill)
              Practice: say "butter" fast, exaggerate the "tt"
              Example: perro (dog), carro (car)
              Minimal pair: pero (but) vs. perro (dog)

J / G(e,i):  Harsh "h" sound from back of throat /x/
              No English equivalent - between "h" and clearing throat
              Example: jugar (to play), gente (people)

LL:          Varies by region:
              - Spain: /ʎ/ (like "million")
              - Latin America: /ʝ/ (like "yes")
              - Argentina/Uruguay: /ʃ/ (like "she")

D (between vowels): Softens to /ð/ like "th" in "this"
              Example: nada /na.ða/, todo /to.ðo/

N before vowel: /ɲ/ like "canyon"
              Example: espanol, ano, manana
```

## Key Grammar Tables

### Ser vs. Estar Decision Guide

```
SER (permanent/inherent)          ESTAR (temporary/state/location)
===========================       ==================================
Identity: Soy profesor            Location: Estoy en casa
Origin: Es de Mexico              Condition: Estoy cansado
Material: Es de madera            Emotion: Estoy feliz
Time: Son las tres                Progressive: Estoy comiendo
Characteristics: Es alto          Result of change: Esta roto
Possession: Es mi libro           (something happened to it)

TRICKY CASES (meaning changes):
  ser aburrido = to be boring (personality)
  estar aburrido = to be bored (current state)

  ser listo = to be clever (trait)
  estar listo = to be ready (state)

  ser malo = to be bad/evil (character)
  estar malo = to be sick (condition)

  ser rico = to be rich (wealth)
  estar rico = to taste delicious (food)
```

### Preterite vs. Imperfect

```
PAST TENSE DECISION GUIDE
============================
PRETERITE (action completed)        IMPERFECT (ongoing/habitual)
  - One-time event                    - Repeated/habitual action
  - Specific start/end                - No clear start/end
  - Sequence of events                - Background/description
  - Interrupting action               - Ongoing action (interrupted)

TRIGGER WORDS:
  Preterite: ayer, anoche, una vez, de repente, el lunes pasado
  Imperfect: siempre, todos los dias, mientras, cuando era nino

EXAMPLE:
  "Cuando era nino (IMPERFECT - background), vivia (IMPERFECT - habitual)
   en Madrid. Un dia (PRETERITE - specific event), mi familia decidio
   (PRETERITE - completed) mudarse a Barcelona."
```

## Vocabulary Building Strategy

```
HIGH-FREQUENCY SPANISH WORD FAMILIES
======================================
Learn these root patterns to multiply your vocabulary:

-CION words (English -tion):
  informacion, educacion, comunicacion, situacion
  (Nearly identical to English - instant vocabulary boost)

-DAD words (English -ty):
  universidad, ciudad, realidad, oportunidad

-MENTE words (English -ly):
  rapidamente, normalmente, exactamente

FALSE FRIENDS (words that look similar but differ):
  embarazada = pregnant (NOT embarrassed)
  actual = current (NOT actual)
  realizar = to carry out (NOT to realize)
  sensible = sensitive (NOT sensible)
  constipado = having a cold (NOT constipated)
  exito = success (NOT exit)
```

## DELE Exam Preparation

```
DELE CERTIFICATION OVERVIEW
=============================
DELE = Diplomas de Espanol como Lengua Extranjera
Issued by: Instituto Cervantes
Recognized: Internationally, for work and academic purposes

LEVELS OFFERED: A1, A2, B1, B2, C1, C2

EXAM STRUCTURE (all levels):
  1. Reading Comprehension (60-90 min)
  2. Listening Comprehension (30-50 min)
  3. Written Expression (45-80 min)
  4. Oral Expression (10-20 min, with preparation time)

PREPARATION TIPS:
  - Use official DELE practice exams from Instituto Cervantes
  - Practice timed writing exercises
  - Record yourself speaking and review
  - Focus on task completion (answering what is asked)
  - Learn exam vocabulary: "redacte" (write), "justifique" (justify)

STUDY TIMELINE:
  B1 DELE: 400-600 hours of total study
  B2 DELE: 600-800 hours of total study
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

```
TOP 10 ENGLISH SPEAKER MISTAKES IN SPANISH
=============================================
1. Using "yo" too much (Spanish drops subject pronouns: "Soy" not "Yo soy")
2. Translating "it" (Spanish often omits: "Es bonito" not "Ello es bonito")
3. Wrong ser/estar choice (study the decision guide above)
4. skipping noun-adjective gender agreement (la casa blancA, not blancO)
5. Adjective placement (usually AFTER the noun: el coche rojo)
6. Overusing "muy" with mucho (say "mucho" not "muy mucho")
7. Using "en" for "on" with days ("el lunes" not "en lunes")
8. Double negatives are CORRECT in Spanish ("No tengo nada" = I have nothing)
9. Confusing por/para (general: por = because of, para = in order to)
10. Not using subjunctive when required (after quiero que, es importante que)
```
