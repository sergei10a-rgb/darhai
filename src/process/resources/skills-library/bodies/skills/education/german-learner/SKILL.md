## CEFR-Aligned Study Path

### A1 - Beginner (0-3 months)

```
A1 CORE OBJECTIVES
====================
Grammar:
  - Present tense of regular and common irregular verbs
  - Sein (to be) and haben (to have)
  - Nominative and accusative cases
  - Definite articles: der/die/das (and their accusative forms)
  - Indefinite articles: ein/eine (and kein/keine for negation)
  - Basic word order: verb in second position (V2 rule)
  - Yes/no questions (verb first) and W-questions
  - Personal pronouns (ich, du, er, sie, es, wir, ihr, sie, Sie)
  - Modal verbs: konnen, mussen, wollen (present tense)
  - Separable verbs: aufstehen, anfangen, einkaufen

Vocabulary targets (500-800 words):
  - Greetings and introductions
  - Numbers, days, months, time
  - Family, food, clothing, colors
  - Directions and places
  - Daily routine verbs

Speaking milestones:
  - Introduce yourself and ask about others
  - Order food and drink
  - Give and understand basic directions
  - Describe your daily routine
```

### A2 - Elementary (3-6 months)

```
A2 CORE OBJECTIVES
====================
Grammar:
  - Dative case (indirect objects, dative prepositions)
  - Perfekt tense (conversational past with haben/sein + past participle)
  - Prateritum of sein and haben (war, hatte)
  - Prepositions with accusative (durch, fur, gegen, ohne, um)
  - Prepositions with dative (aus, bei, mit, nach, seit, von, zu)
  - Two-way prepositions (in, an, auf, uber, unter, vor, hinter, neben, zwischen)
  - Reflexive verbs (sich waschen, sich freuen)
  - Comparative and superlative (grosser, am grossten)
  - Coordinating conjunctions (und, aber, oder, denn, sondern)

Vocabulary targets (1,500-2,000 words):
  - Travel and transportation
  - Health and body parts
  - Work and professions
  - Shopping and money
  - Weather and nature

Speaking milestones:
  - Tell a story about a past experience
  - Make appointments and plans
  - Describe symptoms to a doctor
  - Handle travel situations (hotel, train, airport)
```

### B1 - Intermediate (6-12 months)

```
B1 CORE OBJECTIVES
====================
Grammar:
  - Genitive case (possession, genitive prepositions)
  - Konjunktiv II (conditional: wurde + infinitive, hatte, ware)
  - Passive voice (werden + past participle)
  - Relative clauses (der/die/das as relative pronouns)
  - Subordinating conjunctions (weil, dass, obwohl, wenn, als, damit)
  - Indirect speech (Konjunktiv I basics)
  - Futur I (werden + infinitive)
  - Infinitive clauses (um...zu, ohne...zu, anstatt...zu)

Vocabulary targets (3,000-4,000 words):
  - Abstract concepts (education, politics, environment)
  - Media and technology
  - Feelings and relationships
  - Advanced connectors (deshalb, trotzdem, auserdem, dagegen)

Speaking milestones:
  - Express and defend opinions
  - Discuss current events and social topics
  - Write formal and informal letters/emails
  - Give a short presentation
```

## The German Case System

```
GERMAN CASES EXPLAINED
========================
German has FOUR cases that change articles, pronouns, and adjective endings.

NOMINATIVE (subject - who/what does the action):
  Der Mann liest.        (The man reads.)
  Die Frau singt.        (The woman sings.)
  Das Kind spielt.       (The child plays.)

ACCUSATIVE (direct object - who/what receives the action):
  Ich sehe den Mann.     (I see the man.)
  Ich sehe die Frau.     (I see the woman.)  [no change]
  Ich sehe das Kind.     (I see the child.)  [no change]

DATIVE (indirect object - to/for whom):
  Ich gebe dem Mann das Buch.   (I give the man the book.)
  Ich gebe der Frau das Buch.   (I give the woman the book.)
  Ich gebe dem Kind das Buch.   (I give the child the book.)

GENITIVE (possession - whose):
  Das Buch des Mannes.   (The man's book.)
  Das Buch der Frau.     (The woman's book.)
  Das Buch des Kindes.   (The child's book.)

ARTICLE TABLE:
            Masculine    Feminine    Neuter     Plural
NOM         der          die         das        die
ACC         den          die         das        die
DAT         dem          der         dem        den (+n)
GEN         des (+s/es)  der         des (+s/es) der

QUICK MEMORY AID:
  - Only MASCULINE changes in accusative (der --> den)
  - Dative: "dem, der, dem, den" (m, f, n, pl)
  - Genitive: masculine/neuter nouns add -s or -es
```

## German Word Order

```
WORD ORDER RULES
==================
RULE 1: VERB SECOND (V2) in main clauses
  The conjugated verb is ALWAYS in position 2.

  Ich spiele Tennis.           (I play tennis.)
  Heute spiele ich Tennis.     (Today I play tennis.)
  Tennis spiele ich heute.     (Tennis I play today.)
  [Note: Subject moves, but verb stays in position 2]

RULE 2: VERB FINAL in subordinate clauses
  After subordinating conjunctions, the verb goes to the END.

  Ich weiss, dass er Tennis spielt.
  (I know that he tennis plays.)

  Weil es regnet, bleibe ich zu Hause.
  (Because it rains, stay I at home.)
  [Note: When subordinate clause comes first, main clause
   verb follows immediately = verb-verb at the comma]

RULE 3: VERB BRACKET (Satzklammer)
  When there are two verb parts, they form a bracket:
  Conjugated verb in position 2, other part at the end.

  Ich habe gestern Tennis gespielt.
  (I have yesterday tennis played.)

  Er will morgen nach Berlin fahren.
  (He wants tomorrow to Berlin to-drive.)

RULE 4: TIME - MANNER - PLACE (TMP)
  When multiple adverbs appear, the order is:
  WHEN - HOW - WHERE

  Ich fahre morgen (time) schnell (manner) nach Berlin (place).

COMMON MISTAKE:
  English: "I have not played tennis today."
  Wrong:   "Ich habe nicht gespielt Tennis heute."
  Right:   "Ich habe heute nicht Tennis gespielt."
```

## Compound Words

```
GERMAN COMPOUND WORDS
========================
German forms long compound words by combining shorter words.
The LAST word determines gender, plural, and word class.

FORMATION PATTERN:
  Word 1 + Word 2 = Compound
  Sometimes with linking letters (s, n, en, er)

EXAMPLES:
  Hand + Schuh = Handschuh (glove, literally "hand shoe")
  Kinder + Garten = Kindergarten (children's garden)
  Kranken + Haus = Krankenhaus (hospital, literally "sick house")
  Kuhl + Schrank = Kuhlschrank (refrigerator, literally "cool cabinet")
  Flug + Zeug = Flugzeug (airplane, literally "flying thing")
  Strassen + Bahn = Strassenbahn (streetcar, literally "street track")

LONGER COMPOUNDS:
  Geschwindigkeitsbegrenzung (speed limit)
    Geschwindigkeit (speed) + Begrenzung (limit)

  Rindfleischetikettierungsuberwachungsaufgabenubertragungsgesetz
    (Beef labeling supervision task transfer law)
    -- a real word that appeared in German legislation

HOW TO READ COMPOUNDS:
  1. Read from RIGHT to LEFT for meaning
  2. The last component is the core word
  3. Earlier components modify or specify
  4. Gender comes from the LAST component
     der Schuh (shoe) --> der Handschuh (glove) = masculine
```

## Pronunciation Guide

```
GERMAN PRONUNCIATION
======================
GOOD NEWS: German spelling is highly regular. Once you learn the rules,
you can pronounce almost any word correctly.

VOWELS (long when doubled or followed by single consonant):
  a (long): Vater /faːtɐ/ like "father"
  a (short): Katze /katsə/ like "cut"
  e (long): Meer /meːɐ/ like "may" without glide
  e (short): Bett /bɛt/ like "bet"
  i (long): Lied /liːt/ like "lead"
  i (short): mit /mɪt/ like "mit"
  o (long): Boot /boːt/ like "boat" without glide
  o (short): Gott /ɡɔt/ like British "got"
  u (long): Hut /huːt/ like "hoot"
  u (short): Hund /hʊnt/ like "hood"

UMLAUTS (unique to German):
  a-umlaut: like "e" in "bed"    Madchen, Apfel
  o-umlaut: say "ay" with rounded lips   schon, konnen
  u-umlaut: say "ee" with rounded lips   uber, Tur

CONSONANT RULES:
  ch after a/o/u: "ach" sound (back of throat) /x/ -- Bach, noch
  ch after e/i/umlauts: "ich" sound (like "huge") /ç/ -- ich, Milch
  sch: like English shoe-sound -- Schule, Tisch
  sp/st at start: like shoe-p/shoe-t -- spielen, Strasse
  w: like English "v" -- Wasser, Welt
  v: usually like "f" -- Vater, vier
  z: like "ts" -- Zeit, Zug
  s before vowel: like English "z" -- sagen, Sonne
  ss/eszett: like "s" in "sun" -- Strasse, gross
  final consonant devoicing: d-->t, b-->p, g-->k at end of word
    Hand = /hant/, Tag = /taːk/
```

## Goethe-Zertifikat Preparation

```
GOETHE-ZERTIFIKAT EXAM OVERVIEW
==================================
Issued by: Goethe-Institut
Recognized: Internationally for study, work, and immigration to Germany

LEVELS: A1, A2, B1, B2, C1, C2

EXAM STRUCTURE (B1 as example):
  1. Lesen (Reading): 65 min, 5 parts
  2. Horen (Listening): 40 min, 4 parts
  3. Schreiben (Writing): 60 min, 3 tasks
  4. Sprechen (Speaking): 15 min, 3 parts (paired with another candidate)

SPEAKING SECTION FORMAT (B1):
  Part 1: Plan something together with your partner
  Part 2: Present a topic (given a prompt with bullet points)
  Part 3: Give feedback and ask questions about partner's presentation

WRITING SECTION FORMAT (B1):
  Task 1: Write a message on a forum (about 80 words)
  Task 2: Write a semi-formal email (about 80 words)
  Task 3: Write a formal email (about 80 words)

PREPARATION STRATEGY:
  - Practice with official Goethe-Institut model tests (free online)
  - Practice writing to time limits
  - For speaking: practice both monologue and dialogue formats
  - Vocabulary: learn topic-specific word groups (housing, work, health)
  - Listening: practice with German radio (Deutschlandfunk, slow speed)
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

```
TOP 10 ENGLISH SPEAKER MISTAKES IN GERMAN
============================================
1. Wrong grammatical gender: Always learn the article WITH the noun
   (der Tisch, die Lampe, das Buch -- not just Tisch, Lampe, Buch)

2. Wrong word order: Verb must be in position 2 in main clauses
   Wrong: "Gestern ich habe Tennis gespielt."
   Right: "Gestern habe ich Tennis gespielt."

3. skipping verb-final in subordinate clauses:
   Wrong: "Ich weiss, dass er hat ein Auto."
   Right: "Ich weiss, dass er ein Auto hat."

4. Accusative/Dative confusion with two-way prepositions:
   "in das Restaurant" (motion INTO) vs. "in dem Restaurant" (located IN)
   Movement = accusative, location = dative

5. Wrong past participle formation:
   Regular: ge- + stem + -t (gemacht, gespielt)
   Irregular: ge- + changed stem + -en (gegangen, geschrieben)
   No ge- for: verbs with inseparable prefix (besucht, verstanden)

6. skipping adjective endings: Adjectives change based on case,
   gender, and article type. Study the adjective declension tables.

7. Confusing als/wenn/wann:
   als = when (single past event)
   wenn = when/whenever (repeated or conditional)
   wann = when (question word)

8. Not capitalizing nouns: ALL nouns are capitalized in German.

9. False friends: bekommen = to receive (not "become"),
   Gift = poison (not "gift"), aktuell = current (not "actual")

10. Ignoring separable prefix verbs: The prefix goes to the END.
    "Ich stehe um 7 Uhr auf." (I get up at 7 o'clock.)
```
