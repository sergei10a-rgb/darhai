## Questions to Ask First

1. What is the topic or niche for your community?
2. Who is your ideal member? (Demographics, goals, pain points)
3. Do you have an existing audience? (Email list, social following, current customers)
4. What value will members get that they cannot get for free elsewhere?
5. What is your target price point and how did you arrive at it?
6. How much time per week can you dedicate to community management?
7. What platforms have you considered? (Circle, Skool, Discord, Mighty Networks, Slack)
8. Will you offer multiple tiers or a single membership level?
9. What is your content creation capacity? (Written, video, live events)
10. What does success look like at 6 months? 12 months?

## Platform Selection

### Platform Comparison Matrix
```
CIRCLE:
  Best for: Professional communities, course creators, brands
  Pricing: From $49/month (Starter) to $399/month (Enterprise)
  Strengths: Clean UX, spaces/channels, native courses, event hosting
  Weaknesses: Limited free tier, no built-in discovery
  Integrations: Zapier, webhooks, API access
  Revenue model: You keep 100% of member fees (minus payment processing)

SKOOL:
  Best for: Education-focused communities, coaches
  Pricing: $99/month flat
  Strengths: Gamification, simple UX, built-in courses, discovery page
  Weaknesses: Limited customization, all-or-nothing (one tier)
  Integrations: Limited native integrations
  Revenue model: You keep 100% of member fees

DISCORD:
  Best for: Tech, gaming, younger demographics, real-time chat
  Pricing: Free (Server Boost optional)
  Strengths: Real-time chat, voice channels, bots, free
  Weaknesses: Not designed for paid communities (needs third-party billing),
  overwhelming for non-tech users, content is ephemeral
  Integrations: Extensive bot ecosystem
  Revenue model: Need external billing (LaunchPass, Whop, manual)

MIGHTY NETWORKS:
  Best for: All-in-one community + course + event platform
  Pricing: From $41/month to custom enterprise
  Strengths: Native mobile app, courses, events, livestreaming
  Weaknesses: Higher price, steeper learning curve
  Revenue model: You keep 100% minus payment processing

SLACK:
  Best for: Professional/B2B communities, existing Slack users
  Pricing: Free tier limited, Pro from $8.75/user/month
  Strengths: Familiar interface, threaded conversations, integrations
  Weaknesses: Expensive at scale, not designed for communities,
  messages hidden in channels, no native billing
  Revenue model: Need external billing

DECISION FRAMEWORK:
  If your members are professionals -> Circle or Mighty Networks
  If your focus is education/courses -> Skool or Circle
  If your audience is tech-savvy and wants real-time -> Discord
  If you need mobile-first -> Mighty Networks
  If budget is critical -> Discord (free) + external billing
  If simplicity matters most -> Skool
```

## Tier and Pricing Design

### Pricing Strategy
```
SINGLE TIER (recommended for launch):
  Price: $[X]/month or $[X]/year (annual at 2 months free discount)
  Include everything. Simplify the decision.
  Best for: Communities under 500 members, simple value proposition.

TWO TIERS:
  Community tier: $[X]/month
    Access to community forums, content library, monthly events
  Premium tier: $[XX]/month
    Everything in Community + live coaching, private channels,
    direct access, additional resources

THREE TIERS:
  Community: $[X]/month -- Forum access, content library
  Growth: $[XX]/month -- + Live events, workshops, resources
  Inner Circle: $[XXX]/month -- + 1:1 access, mastermind, priority support

PRICING GUIDELINES:
  B2C communities: $9-49/month is the sweet spot
  B2B communities: $49-299/month (value is higher, budgets exist)
  Premium/mastermind: $99-999/month (high-touch, limited seats)

  Annual pricing: Offer 15-20% discount for annual commitment
  This front-loads revenue and dramatically improves retention.

  Founding member pricing: Launch at 30-50% off "regular" price
  for the first 50-100 members. Grandfather their rate permanently.
  Creates urgency and rewards early adopters.

PRICE ANCHORING:
  "Compare this to [alternative]:
   - Conference ticket: $2,000/year for 3 days of access
   - Online course: $500 one-time for static content
   - Coaching: $300/hour for individual sessions
   - This community: $29/month for ongoing access to all of it"
```

## Content Strategy

### The Content Cadence
```
DAILY (automated or low-effort):
  - Discussion prompt or question of the day
  - Member spotlight or win celebration
  - Curated resource or link share

WEEKLY:
  - One substantial content piece (post, video, lesson)
  - One live event (Q&A, workshop, guest expert, office hours)
  - Community digest email (highlights for those who missed it)

MONTHLY:
  - Deep-dive workshop or masterclass
  - Community challenge or collaborative project
  - Monthly recap and metrics share
  - Member survey or feedback collection

QUARTERLY:
  - Community roadmap update (what is coming)
  - Pricing or tier review
  - Member interviews or case studies
  - Strategic content planning for next quarter

THE 80/20 CONTENT RULE:
  80% of content should come from MEMBERS, not you.
  Your job is to facilitate, not to be a content machine.
  Create frameworks that encourage member contribution:
  - "Share your win this week"
  - "What are you working on?"
  - "Post your biggest challenge and get help from the community"
  - "Introduce yourself using this template"
```

### Content Types That Drive Engagement
```
HIGH ENGAGEMENT:
  1. Live Q&A / Ask Me Anything: Real-time interaction, high value
  2. Challenges: "30-day [topic] challenge" with daily prompts
  3. Accountability groups: Small pods (4-6 people) with shared goals
  4. Hot seats: One member presents, community provides feedback
  5. Coworking sessions: Virtual "work together" with camera on

MEDIUM ENGAGEMENT:
  6. Tutorials and how-tos: Step-by-step content on specific topics
  7. Resource libraries: Templates, tools, frameworks members use
  8. Guest expert sessions: Bring in outside voices for variety
  9. Polls and surveys: Quick engagement, valuable data

LOWER ENGAGEMENT (but still valuable):
  10. Written guides and articles: Reference material
  11. Curated links and news: Industry updates
  12. Recorded workshops: Async content for different time zones
```

## Member Onboarding

### The First 7 Days
```
DAY 0 (IMMEDIATELY AFTER JOINING):
  Automated welcome email:
  - Congratulations on joining
  - Login instructions and platform tour link
  - 3 things to do first:
    1. Introduce yourself in the #introductions channel
    2. Complete your profile (photo, bio, goals)
    3. Join the next live event (link to calendar)
  - "Reply to this email with your biggest question"

DAY 1:
  Personal welcome message (from you or a moderator):
  - "@[Name] Welcome! We are glad you are here."
  - Tag them in a relevant discussion
  - Introduce them to 2-3 members with similar interests

DAY 3:
  Check-in email:
  - "How is your first week going?"
  - Link to the most popular resource or discussion
  - Remind them of the next live event

DAY 7:
  Engagement nudge:
  - If active: "Love seeing you participate! Here are some channels
    you might enjoy..."
  - If inactive: "We noticed you have not posted yet. No pressure,
    but here is an easy way to get started: [prompt]"
  - Invite to an accountability group or small pod

THE ONBOARDING GOAL:
  Get the new member to take THREE ACTIONS in the first 7 days:
  1. Complete their profile
  2. Post an introduction
  3. Comment on someone else's post

  Members who take 3+ actions in week 1 retain at 2-3x the rate
  of members who are passive observers.
```

## Engagement and Retention

### Engagement Systems
```
GAMIFICATION:
  Points/XP: Award points for posting, commenting, attending events
  Levels/Ranks: Member -> Active -> Contributor -> Expert -> Ambassador
  Badges: First post, 30-day streak, helped 10 members, event attendee
  Leaderboards: Top contributors this month (optional, can be demotivating)
  Rewards: Points redeemable for discounts, exclusive content, recognition

RITUALS AND TRADITIONS:
  Weekly: "Win Wednesday" (share your wins), "Feedback Friday"
  Monthly: "Member of the Month" recognition
  Quarterly: Community awards voted by members
  Annual: Virtual summit or anniversary celebration

SMALL GROUP STRUCTURES:
  Accountability pods: 4-6 members meet weekly to share progress
  Mastermind groups: 6-8 members with similar goals, rotating hot seats
  Interest groups: Self-organizing subgroups around specific topics
  Buddy system: Pair new members with experienced ones

ENGAGEMENT METRICS TO TRACK:
  Daily Active Users (DAU) / Monthly Active Users (MAU)
  DAU/MAU ratio: Target 20-30% for healthy community
  Posts per member per month: Target 2-5
  Event attendance rate: Target 15-25% of members
  Response time to new posts: Target < 4 hours
```

### Retention Framework
```
RETENTION METRICS:
  Monthly churn rate: Target < 5% for healthy community
  Annual retention: Target 60-80% (varies by price point)
  Cohort analysis: Track retention by join month

  Retention by engagement level:
    Highly active members: 95%+ monthly retention
    Moderately active: 85-95% retention
    Lurkers (read but do not post): 70-85% retention
    Non-engaged: 40-60% retention

CHURN PREVENTION:
  At-risk signals:
  - No login in 14 days
  - Stopped attending events
  - Reduced posting frequency
  - Payment failure (involuntary churn)

  Intervention triggers:
  - 14 days inactive: Personal message from moderator
  - 30 days inactive: "We miss you" email with highlights of what
    they missed
  - Payment failure: Dunning sequence (3 retry attempts over 7 days,
    then email asking them to update payment)
  - Cancellation request: Exit survey + retention offer
    (discount, pause, downgrade)

EXIT SURVEY QUESTIONS:
  1. What was the primary reason you decided to cancel?
  2. What would have made you stay?
  3. How would you rate your overall experience? (1-10)
  4. Would you consider returning in the future?
  5. Is there anything else you would like us to know?
```

## Scaling Operations

### From 0 to 1,000 Members
```
0-50 MEMBERS: The Personal Phase
  You do everything. Personal welcome messages. Every response.
  The intimacy IS the value at this stage.
  Focus: Get to know every member. Understand their needs deeply.

50-200 MEMBERS: The Systems Phase
  Automate onboarding. Create content templates.
  Recruit 2-3 volunteer moderators from active members.
  Focus: Build the systems that will scale.

200-500 MEMBERS: The Delegation Phase
  Hire a part-time community manager.
  Train moderators with clear guidelines.
  Member-led content starts to exceed your content.
  Focus: Shift from creator to facilitator.

500-1,000 MEMBERS: The Leadership Phase
  Full-time community manager or team.
  Ambassador program: Top members help onboard and engage new ones.
  Multiple active discussion areas and interest groups.
  Focus: Culture maintenance and community health.

1,000+ MEMBERS: The Ecosystem Phase
  Community runs with significant member-generated momentum.
  Your role: Strategic direction, marquee events, culture guardian.
  Consider: Additional revenue streams (events, courses, consulting).
  Focus: Sustainable growth without diluting quality.
```
