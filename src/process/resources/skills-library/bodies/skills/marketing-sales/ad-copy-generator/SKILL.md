# Ad Copy Generator

## When to Use


## Process

1. **Gather requirements.** Ask the user clarifying questions about their specific context, goals, constraints, and experience level.

2. **Analyze the situation.** Review the information provided and identify key factors, challenges, and opportunities relevant to ad copy generator.

3. **Develop the framework.** Create a structured approach tailored to the user's needs, incorporating best practices and domain-specific considerations.

4. **Deliver actionable output.** Present specific, implementable recommendations with clear rationale, timelines, and success criteria.

5. **Address edge cases.** Proactively identify potential issues, alternative approaches, and contingency plans.

**Use this skill when:**
- User needs guidance on ad copy generator
- User asks about ad copy generator best practices or techniques
- User wants a structured approach to ad copy generator

**Do NOT use this skill when:**
- A more specialized skill exists for the specific subtopic
- The request is outside the scope of ad copy generator
## Questions to Ask the User First

1. **What platform(s) are you advertising on?** (Google, Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, YouTube)
2. **What is your product/service?**
3. **Who is your target audience?** (Demographics, interests, behaviors, job titles)
4. **What is your campaign objective?** (Awareness, traffic, leads, sales, app installs)
5. **What is your primary offer?** (Free trial, discount, content download, product)
6. **What is your unique selling proposition?**
7. **What is your budget?** (Daily/monthly)
8. **What landing page will ads link to?**
9. **Do you have any brand guidelines or restrictions?** (Tone, words to avoid)
10. **What has worked or not worked in past ads?**

## Step 2: Facebook/Instagram Ad Copy

### Ad Format Templates
```
FACEBOOK/INSTAGRAM AD COPY TEMPLATES
FORMAT 1: SINGLE IMAGE/VIDEO AD
Primary Text (125 chars visible before "See more"):
  Hook: "{{Stop-the-scroll first line}}"
  Body: "{{Problem statement or benefit expansion}}"
  CTA: "{{Click link/tap below/comment YES}}"
  Character limit: 125 visible, 2200 max
  Hook formulas:
  "Stop {{doing_thing_wrong}} and start {{doing_thing_right}}."
  "{{Number}}% of {{audience}} are making this mistake."
  "What if you could {{desirable_outcome}} in {{timeframe}}?"
  "This is the {{product}} every {{audience}} needs."
Headline (under ad image, 40 chars optimal):
  "{{Primary Benefit or Offer}}"
Description (under headline, 30 chars optimal):
  "{{Supporting detail or urgency}}"
CTA Button: Shop Now / Learn More / Sign Up / Get Offer / Download / Book Now
FORMAT 2: CAROUSEL AD (3-10 cards)
Card 1: Hook/Problem
  Image: {{attention_grabbing_visual}}
  Headline: "{{Problem_statement_or_main_hook}}"
Card 2-4: Benefits/Features
  Image: {{product_feature_visual}}
  Headline: "{{Benefit_statement}}"
  Description: "{{Supporting_detail}}"
Card 5: CTA/Offer
  Image: {{product_or_offer_visual}}
  Headline: "{{CTA_with_offer}}"
  Description: "{{Urgency_or_risk_reducer}}"
FORMAT 3: STORY/REEL AD
Visual: {{Video or image, 9:16 ratio}}
Text overlay: {{5-10 words max, large font}}
CTA: Swipe Up / Shop Now / Learn More
Story ad rules:
- [ ] First 3 seconds must hook (no logo intro)
- [ ] Text overlay is brief (supplements visual, not the point)
- [ ] Sound on (but design for sound off with captions)
- [ ] 15-30 seconds optimal for Stories
- [ ] Under 60 seconds for Reels ads
- [ ] CTA appears in first 5 seconds for short ads
```

### Facebook Ad Copy by Funnel Stage
```
AD COPY BY FUNNEL STAGE

TOP OF FUNNEL (Cold audience, awareness):
  Goal: Stop the scroll, create interest
  Tone: Educational, curiosity-driven
  Offer: Free content, tools, guides

  Template:
  "{{Surprising statistic or bold claim}}.

  Most {{audience}} think {{common_belief}}. But the truth is {{contrarian_insight}}.

  We put together a free {{resource}} that shows you exactly how to {{desirable_outcome}}.

  Tap the link below to get it free."

MIDDLE OF FUNNEL (Warm audience, consideration):
  Goal: Build trust, overcome objections
  Tone: Proof-driven, testimonial-heavy
  Offer: Case studies, demos, webinars, free trials

  Template:
  "{{Customer_name}} was struggling with {{problem}}.

  After trying {{product}}, they {{specific_result}} in just {{timeframe}}.

  '{{Direct customer quote}}' -- {{Name}}, {{Title}}

  Want the same results? Start your free trial today."

BOTTOM OF FUNNEL (Hot audience, conversion):
  Goal: Close the sale, create urgency
  Tone: Direct, urgent, offer-focused
  Offer: Discounts, limited-time offers, final CTAs

  Template:
  "You have been looking at {{product}}. Here is your sign.

  For the next {{timeframe}}, get {{offer}} when you {{action}}.

  {{Number}} people claimed this offer today.

  Do not miss out. Tap below."
```

```

  "Tired of {{problem}}? {{Product}} {{solves it}} in {{timeframe}}."

  "If you {{audience_identifier}}, then you need {{product}}."

  "Get {{specific_benefit}} with {{product}}. {{Risk_reducer}}."

  "The {{topic}} secret that {{impressive_claim}} (it is not what you think)."

FORMULA 10: COMPARISON
```

## Step 7: Compliance Guidelines
```
AD COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST

GOOGLE ADS POLICIES:
- [ ] No misleading or deceptive claims
- [ ] No superlatives without proof ("best", "fastest", "#1")
- [ ] No excessive punctuation or symbols
- [ ] No phone numbers in ad text (use call extension)
- [ ] No trademarks you don't own (unless authorized)
- [ ] Landing page matches ad content (no bait-and-switch)
- [ ] Prohibited categories respected (alcohol, gambling, healthcare vary by region)
- [ ] Correct use of dynamic keyword insertion

FACEBOOK/INSTAGRAM ADS POLICIES:
- [ ] No before/after images implying transformation (health/fitness)
- [ ] No personal attributes ("Are you overweight?" is banned)
- [ ] No misleading health claims
- [ ] No discriminatory targeting (housing, employment, credit)
- [ ] Ad image < 20% text (guideline, not hard rule now)
- [ ] Landing page functions correctly (no broken links)
- [ ] No sensational or fear-mongering content
- [ ] Proper use of "Sponsored by" disclosures

LINKEDIN ADS POLICIES:
- [ ] Professional and accurate content
- [ ] No profanity or offensive material
- [ ] Targeting restrictions for certain categories (jobs, housing)
- [ ] Clear attribution for statistics and claims
- [ ] Privacy policy linked for lead gen forms

GENERAL COMPLIANCE:
- [ ] FTC disclosure: If using testimonials, include typical results
- [ ] FTC disclosure: If using influencers, they must disclose #ad or #sponsored
- [ ] All claims substantiated with evidence
- [ ] Terms and conditions for offers are accessible
- [ ] Privacy policy is current and accessible from landing page
```
