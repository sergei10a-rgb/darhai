# Personal Brand Strategy

## When to Use

- User wants to build or refine their professional personal brand
- User asks how to become known for their expertise
- User wants a plan for increasing professional visibility
- User needs a content strategy tied to career growth
- User asks about thought leadership or establishing authority in their field
- Do NOT use when: user wants LinkedIn profile text written (use `linkedin-summary-writer`), user needs a resume (use `resume-bullet-writer`), user wants business-level social media marketing (use content marketing skills)

## Process

1. **Gather professional identity inputs.** Collect the foundation for the brand:
   - Current role, industry, and years of experience
   - 2-3 areas of deepest expertise or knowledge
   - Career goal for the next 1-3 years (promotion, career change, consulting, speaking, hiring)
   - What the user wants to be known for (the "go-to person for X" statement)
   - Current online presence (LinkedIn, personal site, social media, none)
   - Time available per week for brand-building activities (1 hour, 3 hours, 5+ hours)

2. **Define the unique value proposition (UVP).** Craft the core positioning statement:
   - Intersection formula: [Expertise Area 1] + [Expertise Area 2] + [Unique Perspective] = UVP
   - The UVP must be specific enough to differentiate from thousands of others in the same field
   - Test: "I help [target audience] with [specific problem] by [unique approach]"
   - Identify 2-3 proof points that validate the UVP (achievements, results, experience)

3. **Identify the target audience.** Define who the brand is for:
   - Primary audience: the people whose attention matters most for the career goal
   - Secondary audience: peers and community members who amplify the message
   - What problems does the target audience face that the user can address?
   - Where does the target audience spend time online?
   - What format does the target audience prefer (long-form articles, short posts, video, podcasts)?

4. **Select platforms.** Choose 1-2 primary platforms based on audience and capacity:
   - LinkedIn: best for B2B, professional services, corporate careers, consulting
   - Personal blog or newsletter: best for deep expertise, thought leadership, SEO
   - Industry-specific communities: best for niche expertise and peer recognition
   - Evaluate each platform on: audience presence, content format fit, time investment, discoverability
   - Do NOT recommend spreading across 4+ platforms -- focus beats coverage

5. **Define content themes.** Build a content pillar system:
   - 3-4 content pillars (recurring themes) aligned with the UVP
   - For each pillar: 5-7 specific topic ideas the user can create content about
   - Content mix: 60% educational (teaches something), 25% perspective (opinion or insight), 15% personal (story or experience)
   - Format recommendations per pillar (written post, carousel, case study, how-to)

6. **Build the 90-day visibility plan.** Create a phased execution plan:
   - Days 1-30: Foundation (optimize profiles, establish content cadence, first 4-8 posts)
   - Days 31-60: Consistency (maintain cadence, engage with others' content, join 1-2 conversations per day)
   - Days 61-90: Expansion (guest content, collaborations, community participation, reach measurement)
   - Weekly content calendar with specific posting schedule
   - Engagement targets (comments, connections, conversations per week)

## Rules

1. Always produce a complete brand strategy document with UVP, audience, platforms, content pillars, and 90-day plan -- never generic branding advice
2. The UVP must be specific to the user's intersection of skills and experience -- reject generic statements like "passionate professional who delivers results"
3. NEVER recommend more than 2 primary platforms -- focus and consistency beat broad but shallow presence
4. Content pillar topics must be concrete enough that the user can write about them immediately, not abstract themes like "industry trends"
5. The 90-day plan must have weekly granularity with specific actions, not monthly goals alone
6. Time commitment must be realistic for the user's stated availability -- do not prescribe a 10-hour-per-week plan for someone who has 2 hours available
7. NEVER recommend buying followers, engagement pods, or any artificial growth tactics
8. Include both content creation and engagement activities -- creating without engaging is broadcasting, not brand-building
9. Engagement targets must be specific (e.g., "comment on 3 posts per day from target audience members") not vague ("engage with the community")
10. If the user has no existing online presence, add a "Week 0" setup phase to the plan covering profile creation and initial optimization

## Edge Cases

- **User has no idea what they want to be known for:** Guide them through a discovery exercise: What questions do colleagues come to them for? What topics can they talk about for 30 minutes without preparation? What work are they most proud of? Use answers to draft 2-3 UVP options and let the user choose.
- **User works in a highly regulated industry (finance, healthcare, law):** Add compliance considerations to the content strategy. Note that all content should be reviewed against employer social media policies. Focus on educational and thought-leadership content that shares frameworks and perspectives rather than specific advice or client information.
- **User wants to build a personal brand while job hunting:** Adjust the strategy for dual purpose: attract recruiters and hiring managers while building industry visibility. Front-load content that demonstrates competence and domain knowledge. Add job search signals to the platform strategy (e.g., "Open to Work" on LinkedIn).
- **User has a negative or controversial online history:** Address this directly. Recommend auditing and cleaning up existing profiles before starting new content. The 90-day plan should include reputation recalibration by consistently publishing high-quality content that establishes the desired narrative.
- **User is a freelancer or consultant seeking clients:** Shift the brand strategy from career advancement to client acquisition. Content pillars should address client pain points directly. Include case study and results-oriented content that demonstrates ROI. Add a call-to-action strategy for converting content viewers into leads.

## Example

**Input:** "I'm a data engineer with 6 years of experience. I want to become known as an expert in data pipeline architecture. My goal is to move into a senior or staff engineer role in the next 2 years. I have about 3 hours per week. I have a LinkedIn profile but I've never posted anything."

**Output:**

## Personal Brand Strategy: Data Engineer

**Date:** [Current date]
**Career goal:** Senior/Staff Data Engineer within 2 years
**Time commitment:** 3 hours per week

---

### Unique Value Proposition

**Positioning statement:** I help data teams build reliable, scalable pipelines by sharing practical architecture patterns from 6 years of building production data systems.

**Intersection formula:**
- Expertise 1: Data pipeline architecture and orchestration
- Expertise 2: Production system reliability and failure recovery
- Unique perspective: Practical lessons from building and maintaining real systems, not theoretical best practices

**Proof points:**
1. 6 years of hands-on data engineering across production environments
2. [Specific pipeline or system built -- scale, complexity, business impact]
3. [Specific challenge solved -- migration, reliability improvement, cost reduction]

### Target Audience

**Primary audience:** Hiring managers and senior engineers at companies with complex data infrastructure -- their attention opens doors to senior/staff roles
**Secondary audience:** Mid-level data engineers who will engage with and amplify technical content

**Audience problems the user can address:**
1. How to design pipelines that do not break at 10x scale
2. How to choose between orchestration tools for different use cases
3. How to debug and recover from pipeline failures without data loss

**Where they spend time:** LinkedIn, data engineering communities, technical blogs
**Preferred format:** Short technical posts (LinkedIn), detailed write-ups (blog)

### Platform Strategy

**Primary platform:** LinkedIn -- highest concentration of hiring managers and senior engineers in data
**Secondary platform:** Personal blog or newsletter -- long-form technical depth for SEO and portfolio

| Platform | Purpose | Content Type | Posting Frequency |
|----------|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| LinkedIn | Primary visibility, hiring manager reach | Short technical posts (200-400 words) | 2 per week |
| Personal blog | Depth, SEO, portfolio | Long-form technical write-ups (800-1500 words) | 1 per month |

### Content Pillars

**Pillar 1: Pipeline Architecture Patterns** -- Specific patterns for common data pipeline challenges
- Topic ideas: batch vs. streaming decision framework, idempotent pipeline design, schema evolution strategies, backfill architecture, multi-tenant pipeline isolation
- Best format: LinkedIn post with diagram or code snippet

**Pillar 2: Reliability and Failure Recovery** -- How to build pipelines that recover gracefully
- Topic ideas: circuit breaker patterns for data pipelines, monitoring and alerting strategies, dead letter queue design, data quality gates, incident post-mortem lessons
- Best format: LinkedIn post (short) + blog post (detailed case study)

**Pillar 3: Lessons from Production** -- Real stories from building and maintaining data systems
- Topic ideas: the migration that taught me about testing, scaling from 1M to 100M rows per day, why I stopped using [approach] and switched to [approach], the cost optimization that saved $X per month, debugging a silent data corruption issue
- Best format: LinkedIn narrative post (personal story format)

**Content mix target:** 60% educational | 25% perspective | 15% personal

---

### 90-Day Visibility Plan

#### Week 0: Setup (Before Day 1)

- Optimize LinkedIn profile: headline includes "Data Engineer | Pipeline Architecture," about section states UVP
- Professional headshot if not already present
- Set up blog platform with first draft post in progress

#### Days 1-30: Foundation

| Week | Actions | Deliverables |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Week 1 | Write and publish first LinkedIn post (Pillar 1 topic), comment on 3 data engineering posts per day | 1 LinkedIn post published, 15 comments |
| Week 2 | Write and publish second LinkedIn post (Pillar 3 story), connect with 10 data engineers | 1 LinkedIn post, 10 connection requests |
| Week 3 | Publish first blog post (detailed Pillar 1 topic), share on LinkedIn | 1 blog post, 1 LinkedIn share post |
| Week 4 | Write 2 LinkedIn posts (Pillar 1 and Pillar 2), continue daily commenting | 2 LinkedIn posts, 15 comments |

**Month 1 targets:** 5 LinkedIn posts, 1 blog post, 60 comments on others' content, 20 new connections

#### Days 31-60: Consistency

| Week | Actions | Deliverables |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Week 5 | 2 LinkedIn posts, start engaging with data engineering community leaders | 2 posts, identify 5 key voices to engage with |
| Week 6 | 2 LinkedIn posts, publish second blog post | 2 LinkedIn posts, 1 blog post |
| Week 7 | 2 LinkedIn posts, respond to every comment on own posts within 24 hours | 2 posts, 100% comment response rate |
| Week 8 | 2 LinkedIn posts, connect with 10 senior engineers or hiring managers | 2 posts, 10 targeted connections |

**Month 2 targets:** 8 LinkedIn posts, 1 blog post, 60 comments, 20 new targeted connections

#### Days 61-90: Expansion

| Week | Actions | Deliverables |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Week 9 | 2 LinkedIn posts, pitch a guest post to a data engineering publication or newsletter | 2 posts, 1 pitch sent |
| Week 10 | 2 LinkedIn posts, publish third blog post, share a post that references another creator's work | 2 LinkedIn posts, 1 blog post, 1 collaborative mention |
| Week 11 | 2 LinkedIn posts, participate in a data engineering discussion thread or community event | 2 posts, 1 community contribution |
| Week 12 | 2 LinkedIn posts, 90-day review and strategy adjustment | 2 posts, written review of metrics vs. targets |

**Month 3 targets:** 8 LinkedIn posts, 1 blog post, 1 guest or collaborative content, measurable inbound interest

---

### Success Metrics (90-Day Review)

| Metric | Target | How to Measure |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Posts published (LinkedIn) | 21 | Count |
| Blog posts published | 3 | Count |
| Engagement rate | 3-5% | Likes + comments per post / impressions |
| New connections | 60 | LinkedIn network growth |
| Inbound messages or opportunities | 3+ | Recruiter messages, collaboration invites, referrals |
| Profile views | 50% increase | LinkedIn analytics comparison month 1 vs. month 3 |
