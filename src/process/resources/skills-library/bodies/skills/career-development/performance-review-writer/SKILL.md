# Performance Review Writer
## When to Use

**Use this skill when:**
- User asks about performance review writer
- User needs guidance on performance review writer topics
- User wants a structured approach to performance review writer

**Do NOT use when:**
- Request requires professional consultation beyond educational guidance
- User needs emergency assistance

## Questions to Ask the User First

1. **What type of review is this?** (annual, semi-annual, quarterly, probationary, promotion-focused)
2. **When is the review due?**
3. **What format does your company use?** (free-form, structured form, rating scales, competency-based)
4. **What were your goals from the last review period?**
5. **What are your top 3-5 achievements since the last review?**
6. **Are you aiming for a promotion or raise with this review?**
7. **Do you have metrics or quantified results to reference?**
8. **Have you received any notable feedback (positive or constructive) during the period?**
9. **Are there any challenges or setbacks you need to address?**
10. **What are your career aspirations for the next 1-2 years?**

## Step 1: Achievement Documentation (CAR Method)

### The CAR Framework
```
CAR METHOD:

C - CONTEXT: What was the situation or challenge?
A - ACTION: What did YOU specifically do?
R - RESULT: What was the measurable outcome?

WHY CAR (not STAR):
Self-assessments do not need the separate Task element that
interview answers do. CAR is more concise for written reviews.
```

### CAR Achievement Worksheet
```
ACHIEVEMENT DOCUMENTATION TEMPLATE:

Achievement #{{NUMBER}}:

CONTEXT:
"During {{TIME_PERIOD}}, our team faced {{CHALLENGE/OPPORTUNITY}}.
{{ADDITIONAL_CONTEXT_1-2_SENTENCES}}."

ACTION:
"I {{SPECIFIC_ACTION_1}}.
I also {{SPECIFIC_ACTION_2}}.
This involved {{DETAILS_THAT_SHOW_COMPLEXITY_OR_INITIATIVE}}."

RESULT:
"As a result, {{QUANTIFIED_OUTCOME}}.
This contributed to {{BROADER_BUSINESS_IMPACT}}.
{{OPTIONAL: This was recognized by / This established a new standard for}}."

CONSOLIDATED VERSION (for the actual review):
"{{ACTION_VERB}} {{WHAT_YOU_DID}}, resulting in {{QUANTIFIED_RESULT}}.
{{ONE_SENTENCE_ON_BROADER_IMPACT}}."
```

### Achievement Categories to Consider
```
ACHIEVEMENT CATEGORIES:

Review each category -- you likely have achievements in several:

BUSINESS IMPACT:
[ ] Revenue generated or influenced
[ ] Costs reduced or avoided
[ ] Efficiency improvements (time saved, process improved)
[ ] Customer satisfaction improvements
[ ] Risk mitigation

PROJECT DELIVERY:
[ ] Projects completed on time and on budget
[ ] New products or features launched
[ ] Process or system implementations
[ ] Successful migrations or upgrades

LEADERSHIP:
[ ] Team members mentored or developed
[ ] Cross-functional initiatives led
[ ] Hiring contributions (interviews, onboarding)
[ ] Culture or morale improvements

INNOVATION:
[ ] New ideas implemented
[ ] Problems solved creatively
[ ] Processes or tools introduced
[ ] Experiments or pilots run

PROFESSIONAL GROWTH:
[ ] New skills acquired
[ ] Certifications earned
[ ] Presentations or publications
[ ] Expanded scope of responsibility

COLLABORATION:
[ ] Cross-team partnerships
[ ] Stakeholder relationship building
[ ] Knowledge sharing (documentation, training)
[ ] Helping others succeed
```

## Step 2: Goal Reflection

### Reviewing Last Period's Goals
```
GOAL REFLECTION TEMPLATE:

GOAL 1: {{ORIGINAL_GOAL}}
Status: EXCEEDED / MET / PARTIALLY MET / NOT MET
What happened: {{BRIEF_NARRATIVE}}
Metric: {{TARGET}} vs {{ACTUAL}}
Key factors: {{WHAT_CONTRIBUTED_TO_THE_OUTCOME}}
Learning: {{WHAT_YOU_LEARNED}}

GOAL 2: {{ORIGINAL_GOAL}}
Status: EXCEEDED / MET / PARTIALLY MET / NOT MET
What happened: {{BRIEF_NARRATIVE}}
Metric: {{TARGET}} vs {{ACTUAL}}
Key factors: {{WHAT_CONTRIBUTED_TO_THE_OUTCOME}}
Learning: {{WHAT_YOU_LEARNED}}

GOAL 3: {{ORIGINAL_GOAL}}
Status: EXCEEDED / MET / PARTIALLY MET / NOT MET
What happened: {{BRIEF_NARRATIVE}}
Metric: {{TARGET}} vs {{ACTUAL}}
Key factors: {{WHAT_CONTRIBUTED_TO_THE_OUTCOME}}
Learning: {{WHAT_YOU_LEARNED}}

UNPLANNED ACCOMPLISHMENTS:
1. {{ACCOMPLISHMENT}}
2. {{ACCOMPLISHMENT}}
```

### Framing Goals You Did Not Fully Meet
```
ADDRESSING MISSED GOALS:

DO frame it as:
- What you learned and how you adjusted
- What partial progress was made
- What factors were outside your control (with specifics)
- What you are doing differently going forward

DO NOT:
- Ignore missed goals (your manager will notice)
- Make excuses without taking ownership
- Blame others entirely

TEMPLATE:
"While I did not fully achieve {{GOAL}} (reached {{ACTUAL}} vs
target of {{TARGET}}), I made meaningful progress by {{WHAT_YOU_DID}}.
The primary challenge was {{HONEST_ASSESSMENT}}. Going forward,
I am addressing this by {{SPECIFIC_ACTION}}, and I am confident
this will result in {{EXPECTED_OUTCOME}}."
```

## Step 3: SMART Goal Setting for Next Period

### SMART Goal Framework
```
SMART GOAL TEMPLATE:

S - SPECIFIC: What exactly will you accomplish?
M - MEASURABLE: How will you know it is done? What is the metric?
R - RELEVANT: Does this align with team/company priorities?
T - TIME-BOUND: When will this be completed?

GOAL TEMPLATE:
"By {{DATE}}, I will {{SPECIFIC_ACTION}} measured by {{METRIC}},
which supports {{TEAM/COMPANY_OBJECTIVE}}."

WEAK: "Improve customer satisfaction"
SMART: "Increase NPS score from 42 to 55 by Q4 2026 by implementing
a new customer feedback loop and response protocol."

WEAK: "Learn more about data analytics"
SMART: "Complete the Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate
by June 2026 and apply skills to build a team performance dashboard
used in weekly leadership reviews."

WEAK: "Be a better leader"
SMART: "Increase team engagement score from 3.6 to 4.2 (out of 5)
by December 2026 by implementing monthly 1-on-1s, quarterly team
retrospectives, and a peer recognition program."
```

### Goal Categories
```
BALANCED GOAL SETTING:

Set 4-6 goals across these categories:

1. PERFORMANCE GOAL (core job execution)
   "{{SMART_GOAL}}"

2. GROWTH GOAL (skill development)
   "{{SMART_GOAL}}"

3. TEAM/LEADERSHIP GOAL (collaboration, mentoring)
   "{{SMART_GOAL}}"

4. INNOVATION GOAL (improving processes, tools, outcomes)
   "{{SMART_GOAL}}"

5. CAREER DEVELOPMENT GOAL (positioning for next role)
   "{{SMART_GOAL}}"

manager's stated objectives. Ask your manager: "What would make
the biggest impact for the team this year?"
```

## Step 4: Strength and Development Area Framing

### Positioning Strengths
```
STRENGTH FRAMING TEMPLATE:

Strength: {{STRENGTH}}

Evidence: "This was demonstrated when I {{SPECIFIC_EXAMPLE_WITH_RESULT}}.
Colleagues and stakeholders have noted {{FEEDBACK_QUOTE_OR_PARAPHRASE}}.
I plan to continue leveraging this strength by {{HOW_YOU_WILL_APPLY_IT}}."

"My ability to work effectively across teams was particularly evident
in the Q3 platform migration, where I coordinated between engineering,
product, and customer success to deliver on time with zero customer
impact. Our VP of Engineering noted that the communication cadence
I established 'set a new standard for cross-team projects.' I plan
to continue leveraging this strength by taking on the upcoming
integration initiative."
```

### Positioning Development Areas
```
DEVELOPMENT AREA FRAMING:

RULE: Always pair the development area with a concrete action plan.

TEMPLATE:
"I have identified {{DEVELOPMENT_AREA}} as an area for growth.
Specifically, I want to improve my ability to {{SPECIFIC_BEHAVIOR}}.
I have already begun addressing this by {{ACTION_TAKEN}}. My plan
for the next period includes {{SPECIFIC_STEPS}}, and I will measure
progress by {{METRIC_OR_MILESTONE}}."

DEVELOPMENT AREAS THAT SHOW SELF-AWARENESS:
- Technical skill you are actively building
- Leadership capability you are developing
- Communication skill you are refining
- Strategic thinking you are expanding

DEVELOPMENT AREAS TO AVOID MENTIONING:
- Anything that is a core requirement of your current role
- Anything that suggests you cannot do your job
- Vague statements without action plans ("I need to be better at...")
```

## Step 6: 360 Feedback Preparation

### Requesting 360 Feedback
```
360 FEEDBACK REQUEST:

"Hi {{NAME}},

I am preparing for my performance review and would really value
your perspective. Would you be willing to share brief feedback
on the following?

1. What do you see as my key strengths?
2. What is one area where I could improve?
3. How effectively do I {{SPECIFIC_COMPETENCY_RELEVANT_TO_THEM}}?

Any specific examples would be especially helpful. I am happy to
reciprocate if you would like feedback as well.

Thanks so much,
{{YOUR_NAME}}"

WHO TO ASK:
[ ] 2-3 peers you work closely with
[ ] 1-2 stakeholders in other departments
[ ] 1-2 direct reports (if applicable)
[ ] 1 senior leader who has visibility into your work
```

### Incorporating 360 Feedback
```
INCORPORATING FEEDBACK:

When referencing peer/stakeholder feedback in your review:

"Feedback from colleagues has highlighted {{STRENGTH}}, with
{{NAME}} noting that '{{QUOTE_OR_PARAPHRASE}}.' I have also
received constructive feedback about {{AREA}}, which I am
addressing by {{SPECIFIC_ACTION}}."

Third-party validation is more powerful than self-assessment alone.
```

## Step 7: Building a Case for Promotion

### Promotion Case Framework
```
PROMOTION CASE BUILDER:

TARGET ROLE: {{NEXT_LEVEL_TITLE}}

STEP 1: UNDERSTAND THE REQUIREMENTS
[ ] Get the job description or leveling guide for the target role
[ ] Identify the key competencies expected at the next level
[ ] Ask your manager: "What does it take to get promoted to {{LEVEL}}?"
[ ] Talk to people who recently got promoted to that level

STEP 2: MAP YOUR EVIDENCE

Competency: {{COMPETENCY_1}} (e.g., "Strategic Thinking")
Next Level Expectation: {{WHAT_IS_EXPECTED}}
My Evidence: {{SPECIFIC_EXAMPLE_SHOWING_YOU_ALREADY_DO_THIS}}

Competency: {{COMPETENCY_2}}
Next Level Expectation: {{WHAT_IS_EXPECTED}}
My Evidence: {{SPECIFIC_EXAMPLE}}

Competency: {{COMPETENCY_3}}
Next Level Expectation: {{WHAT_IS_EXPECTED}}
My Evidence: {{SPECIFIC_EXAMPLE}}

STEP 3: IDENTIFY AND CLOSE GAPS
Gap: {{SKILL_OR_EXPERIENCE_NEEDED}}
Plan to close: {{ACTION}} by {{DATE}}

STEP 4: BUILD ADVOCATES
[ ] Your direct manager (must be your champion)
[ ] Skip-level manager (visibility matters)
[ ] Key stakeholders who can vouch for your impact
[ ] HR/People partner (understand the process)

STEP 5: DOCUMENT THE CASE

PROMOTION CASE DOCUMENT:
"I am requesting consideration for promotion to {{ROLE}} based on
the following:

SCOPE INCREASE:
My responsibilities have grown to include {{EXPANDED_SCOPE}}, which
is aligned with {{NEXT_LEVEL}} expectations.

IMPACT AT NEXT LEVEL:
I have been consistently operating at the {{NEXT_LEVEL}} by:
1. {{EXAMPLE_OF_NEXT_LEVEL_WORK_WITH_METRIC}}
2. {{EXAMPLE_OF_NEXT_LEVEL_WORK_WITH_METRIC}}
3. {{EXAMPLE_OF_NEXT_LEVEL_WORK_WITH_METRIC}}

PEER COMPARISON:
My contributions are on par with or exceed those of current
{{NEXT_LEVEL_TITLE}}s on the team, as evidenced by {{EVIDENCE}}.

STAKEHOLDER ENDORSEMENT:
{{NAME}} has noted: '{{QUOTE}}'
{{NAME}} has noted: '{{QUOTE}}'

FUTURE COMMITMENT:
At the {{NEXT_LEVEL}}, I plan to {{VALUE_YOU_WILL_DELIVER}}."
```

### Promotion Conversation Script
```
PROMOTION DISCUSSION WITH MANAGER:

"{{MANAGER_NAME}}, I would like to discuss my career progression.
Over the past {{TIME_PERIOD}}, I have {{BRIEF_SUMMARY_OF_IMPACT}}.

I believe my work has been consistently at the {{NEXT_LEVEL}} level,
particularly in areas like {{COMPETENCY_1}} and {{COMPETENCY_2}}.

I would like to work toward a promotion to {{TITLE}}. Can we
discuss what that would look like and what I need to do to make
that happen in the next {{TIMELINE}}?"

FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS:
- "What specific criteria need to be met for promotion?"
- "What gaps do you see between where I am and the next level?"
- "Who else needs to be involved in the promotion decision?"
- "What timeline is realistic?"
- "What can I do in the next 90 days to strengthen my case?"
```

## Review Writing Best Practices
```
SELF-EVALUATION DO'S AND DON'TS:

DO:
+ Use specific metrics and examples (not vague claims)
+ Write in confident, professional tone
+ Take credit for your contributions clearly
+ Reference feedback from others
+ Align achievements to company/team goals
+ Show self-awareness about development areas
+ Propose goals that align with business needs
+ Proofread thoroughly

DON'T:
- Undersell your contributions (this is not the time for modesty)
- Use vague language ("I did a good job on the project")
- List tasks instead of achievements
- Ignore goals you did not meet
- Compare yourself negatively to colleagues
- Use emotional language
- Wait until the last minute to write it
- skip to track achievements throughout the year
```

## Edge Cases

- **Incomplete information:** Ask clarifying questions before proceeding. Do not assume details the user has not provided.
- **Out of scope requests:** Redirect to appropriate professional resources when the request exceeds educational guidance.
- **Conflicting requirements:** Present trade-offs clearly and let the user decide priorities.
