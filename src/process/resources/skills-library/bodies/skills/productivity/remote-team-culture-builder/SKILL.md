## Async-First Communication

### The Async-First Manifesto

```
Default to async. Meetings are the exception, not the rule.

Async communication:
  - Slack/Teams messages
  - Written proposals and RFCs
  - Recorded video updates (Loom)
  - Shared documents with comments

Sync communication (use only when needed):
  - Brainstorming complex problems
  - Sensitive conversations (feedback, conflict)
  - Relationship building
  - Urgent incidents

Rule of thumb: If it can be a document, it should be a document.
If it can be an async message, don't schedule a meeting.
If it must be a meeting, keep it under 30 minutes with a clear agenda.
```

### Communication Norms Document

```markdown
## Our Communication Norms

### Response Time Expectations
- Slack DMs: Within 4 working hours
- Channel messages: Within 8 working hours (same business day)
- Email: Within 24 working hours
- Urgent (P1 incident): Use PagerDuty/phone

### Channel Usage
- #team-engineering: Team-specific discussions and decisions
- #standup: Async daily updates (what I did, what I'm doing, blockers)
- #wins: Share accomplishments, celebrate each other
- #random: Non-work chat, memes, life updates
- #help-[topic]: Cross-team questions and support

### Meeting Protocol
- Every meeting has an agenda (shared 24h before)
- Every meeting has notes (shared within 2 hours after)
- Optional attendance is genuinely optional
- Camera-on is encouraged but never required
- Record meetings for those who can't attend

### Timezone Respect
- Don't expect responses outside someone's working hours
- Use scheduled send for messages to other timezones
- Core overlap hours: [define for your team]
- Rotate meeting times to share timezone burden fairly
```

## Documentation Culture

### Why Documentation Matters More Remotely

```
In an office: Knowledge transfers through overheard conversations,
whiteboard sessions, hallway chats, and osmosis.

Remote: If it's not written down, it doesn't exist.

What to document:
  - Decisions and their reasoning (not just the decision)
  - Processes and how-tos (onboarding, deployments, reviews)
  - Project context (why, not just what)
  - Team norms and expectations
  - Tribal knowledge (those things "everyone just knows")

Documentation hierarchy:
  1. Source of truth (Notion/Confluence): Long-lived reference
  2. Decision records (RFC/ADR): Why we chose X over Y
  3. Async updates (Slack/Loom): Ephemeral but searchable
  4. Meeting notes: Decisions and actions from sync sessions

Golden rule: Write for the person who joins the team in 6 months
and needs to understand why things are the way they are.
```

### RFC (Request for Comments) Process

```markdown
## RFC Template

**Title:** [Descriptive title]
**Author:** [Name]
**Date:** [Date]
**Status:** Draft / Open for Comment / Accepted / Rejected

### Context
What is the current situation? Why are we considering a change?

### Proposal
What specifically are you proposing?

### Alternatives Considered
What other options did you evaluate? Why were they rejected?

### Impact
Who is affected? What changes for them?

### Open Questions
What are you unsure about? Where do you want input?

### Decision
[Filled in after discussion period]
Decision: [Accept/Reject/Modify]
Decided by: [Name/Group]
Date: [Date]

---
Comments: [Team members add comments below]
```

## Virtual Rituals

### Weekly Rhythms

```
Monday: Weekly Kickoff (async or 15-min sync)
  - Each person shares their top 3 priorities for the week
  - Manager shares team-level context and updates
  - Format: Slack thread or brief standup

Tuesday-Thursday: Focused Work + Async Check-ins
  - Async standup in #standup channel (daily)
  - Format: "Yesterday: / Today: / Blockers:"
  - Or use a bot (Geekbot, Standuply) to prompt at each person's morning

Friday: Week in Review + Social Time
  - 15-min "Wins of the Week" sync (everyone shares one accomplishment)
  - Optional 30-min social hangout (no work talk)
  - Manager shares "What I learned this week" in Slack
```

### Monthly Rituals

```
Team Retrospective (monthly, 45 min):
  - What went well? What could improve? What will we try?
  - Use a retro tool (EasyRetro, Miro, FigJam)
  - Rotate facilitator to distribute ownership
  - Follow up on action items from previous retro

Show and Tell (monthly, 30 min):
  - Each person demos something they built, learned, or are proud of
  - Builds cross-visibility and appreciation
  - Record for absent team members

1:1s (weekly or biweekly, 30 min):
  - Not status updates (use async for that)
  - Focus on: career growth, blockers, feedback, well-being
  - Manager asks: "What's on your mind?" not "What did you do?"
  - Employee-driven agenda encouraged
```

## Team Bonding Activities

### Activities That Actually Work Remotely

```
Low-effort, high-connection:
  - Coffee roulette: Random 1:1 pairings for 15-min non-work chat (Donut bot)
  - Photo of the week: Share one photo from your week in #random
  - Music sharing: Collaborative Spotify playlist updated weekly
  - Book/podcast club: Monthly, voluntary, diverse topics

Medium-effort:
  - Virtual lunch: Company-sponsored meal delivery + group video
  - Show your workspace: Quick video tour (people love seeing setups)
  - Skill share: Team members teach each other something non-work
  - Pet parade: Show off pets on camera (never fails)

Special occasions:
  - Birthday/anniversary celebrations in team channel
  - Milestone celebrations (work anniversaries, promotions, launches)
  - Holiday celebrations that respect diverse backgrounds
  - Personal wins channel (new home, marathon, baby, etc.)

In-person (if budget allows):
  - Annual or semi-annual team offsite (2-3 days)
  - Focus on bonding, not work (work can happen async)
  - Mix structured activities with free time
  - Budget: $1,500-3,000 per person per offsite
```

## Feedback in Remote Teams

### Feedback Mechanisms

```
Continuous feedback:
  - Normalize real-time feedback in 1:1s
  - Use praise publicly, constructive feedback privately
  - "I noticed [behavior]. The impact was [effect]. Could you try [alternative]?"

Structured feedback:
  - Quarterly peer feedback (lightweight, 3-5 questions)
  - Annual 360 review (more comprehensive)
  - Project retrospectives (team-level learnings)

Tools for remote feedback:
  - 15Five: Continuous performance management
  - Lattice: Reviews, goals, and engagement
  - Simple Google Form: Quick pulse surveys
  - Direct Slack DM: For timely, specific feedback

Remote-specific feedback challenges:
  - Harder to read body language (default to over-communicating intent)
  - Written feedback can feel harsher (add context and warmth)
  - Delayed feedback loses impact (give it within 48 hours)
  - Positive feedback is even more important remotely (no hallway high-fives)
```

## Timezone Management

### Strategies for Global Teams

```
No timezone overlap (hardest):
  - Fully async work with recorded handoffs
  - "Follow the sun" model for continuous progress
  - Shared async standup with daily summaries
  - Occasional rotating sync meetings (everyone takes turns with bad times)

Partial overlap (2-4 hours):
  - Concentrate sync activities in overlap window
  - Use overlap for: decision-making, pairing, relationship building
  - Everything else: async with clear handoff documentation

Near-full overlap (6+ hours):
  - Most flexible -- can operate almost like co-located
  - Still default to async for documentation benefits
  - Sync for collaboration and relationship

Timezone equity rules:
  - Never make the same timezone always take the bad meeting time
  - Rotate meeting times monthly or quarterly
  - Record everything for those who can't attend
  - Make decisions in writing, not in meetings alone
  - Consider "timezone tax" -- extra rest/flexibility for people with poor overlap
```

## Measuring Culture Health

```
Quarterly pulse survey (5-7 questions, anonymous):

1. "I feel connected to my team" (1-5)
2. "I have the information I need to do my work" (1-5)
3. "I feel safe sharing my opinions" (1-5)
4. "My contributions are recognized" (1-5)
5. "I can disconnect from work during non-working hours" (1-5)
6. "I would recommend this team to a friend" (NPS, 1-10)
7. "What one thing would improve your experience?" (free text)

Healthy team benchmarks:
  - Average score > 4.0 on all questions
  - eNPS > 30
  - Response rate > 80%
  - Free text themes are constructive, not frustrated

Warning signs:
  - Scores declining over 2+ quarters
  - Low response rate (disengagement)
  - Consistent themes around isolation or burnout
  - High turnover from specific timezones or sub-teams
```

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

```
DON'T:
  - Require cameras on all day (surveillance culture)
  - Use activity monitoring software (destroys trust)
  - Schedule meetings during someone's evening/night
  - Assume silence means agreement (ask explicitly)
  - Only celebrate work wins (people are more than their output)
  - Mandate fun (forced social activities backfire)
  - Make decisions in meetings without written follow-up
  - Ignore timezone equity

DO:
  - Trust people to manage their own time
  - Judge output, not activity
  - Over-communicate context (no one overhears things remotely)
  - Create multiple avenues for connection (not just work channels)
  - Respect boundaries around working hours
  - Document decisions so everyone has equal access to information
  - Regularly ask "Is this meeting necessary?" (often it's not)
```
