## Space Selection Framework

### Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | What to Assess |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Internet quality | 25% | Speed test (minimum 100 Mbps), redundancy, stability |
| Noise management | 20% | Quiet zones, phone booths, noise isolation |
| Location/commute | 15% | Transit access, parking, neighborhood amenities |
| Community fit | 15% | Member demographics, industry mix, vibe |
| Amenities | 10% | Meeting rooms, printing, kitchen, showers, mail |
| Price/value | 10% | Cost per day, included extras, flexibility |
| Hours/access | 5% | 24/7 access, weekend availability |

### Space Type Comparison

```
HOT DESK (shared, unassigned seating):
  Cost: $150-350/month
  Best for: Flexible schedule, budget-conscious, variety seekers
  Tradeoff: No guaranteed spot, must set up daily, less personal

DEDICATED DESK (assigned, permanent):
  Cost: $300-600/month
  Best for: Daily users who want consistency, need monitors/equipment
  Tradeoff: Higher cost, less flexibility, may be in shared area

PRIVATE OFFICE (enclosed room):
  Cost: $500-2,000+/month
  Best for: Teams, frequent calls, need for privacy/quiet
  Tradeoff: Highest cost, less community interaction

DAY PASS:
  Cost: $20-50/day
  Best for: Occasional use, travelers, testing spaces
  Tradeoff: No community building, no guaranteed availability

VIRTUAL MEMBERSHIP:
  Cost: $50-150/month
  Best for: Professional address, occasional day access, networking events
  Tradeoff: Limited physical access
```

### Trial Period Checklist

```
Before committing to a membership, test for one week:

Day 1 (Monday): Arrive at peak time
  [ ] Is there seating available when you arrive?
  [ ] How loud is the space at peak hours?
  [ ] Run speed test at your desk (download, upload, ping)
  [ ] Try the coffee (you will be drinking a lot of it)

Day 2 (Tuesday): Test work infrastructure
  [ ] Book a meeting room -- is it available when you need it?
  [ ] Test printing/scanning if needed
  [ ] Try a phone/video call -- is call quality acceptable?
  [ ] Test phone booth availability during peak call hours

Day 3 (Wednesday): Assess community
  [ ] Attend a community event or lunch
  [ ] Talk to 3 members -- are they in relevant industries?
  [ ] Observe the general vibe -- focused, social, or chaotic?
  [ ] Is staff helpful and responsive?

Day 4-5: Test productivity
  [ ] Can you sustain 4+ hours of deep focus work?
  [ ] Are there quiet zones that actually stay quiet?
  [ ] Is the temperature and lighting comfortable?
  [ ] Do you look forward to going back?
```

## Productivity Rituals

### The Coworking Day Structure

```
Arrival Ritual (15 minutes):
  1. Same desk/area each day (consistency triggers focus)
  2. Set up workspace (monitor, keyboard, notebook, water)
  3. Put on headphones (signal to others: focused)
  4. Review today's 3 priorities
  5. Close all non-essential browser tabs and apps
  6. Start first deep work block

Morning (9-12): Deep Work Phase
  - Most cognitively demanding tasks
  - Headphones on, notifications off
  - No meetings if possible
  - Take a 10-minute break every 90 minutes

Lunch (12-1): Community Time
  - Eat in the common area (not at your desk)
  - Join the communal table
  - This is your networking time -- be open and curious
  - Attend any lunchtime events

Afternoon (1-3): Collaborative Phase
  - Meetings, calls, collaborative work
  - Use meeting rooms for video calls (not your desk)
  - Check and respond to messages

Late Afternoon (3-5): Administrative Phase
  - Email, planning, lighter tasks
  - Use this time for community engagement
  - Wrap up and plan tomorrow before leaving

Departure Ritual (5 minutes):
  - Pack up completely (hot desk) or organize desk (dedicated)
  - Write tomorrow's top 3 priorities
  - Clean up any shared spaces you used
```

## Networking Strategy

### The 80/20 Rule of Coworking Networking

```
80% of networking value comes from 20% of interactions.

High-value networking:
  - Regular lunch conversations with 3-5 people in complementary fields
  - Attending community events consistently (not sporadically)
  - Offering help before asking for anything
  - Following up on conversations with value (article, introduction)

Low-value networking:
  - Collecting business cards without genuine connection
  - Pitching your services to everyone you meet
  - Only attending "networking events" without day-to-day relationships
  - Being present physically but never engaging socially
```

### Building Relationships

```
Week 1-2: Observe and Orient
  - Learn people's names and what they do
  - Be friendly but not pushy
  - Ask genuine questions about their work
  - Show up consistently at the same times

Month 1: Establish Presence
  - Join recurring events (lunch clubs, skill shares, happy hours)
  - Offer to help someone with something you're good at
  - Introduce yourself to community managers
  - Share interesting articles or resources

Month 2-3: Deepen Connections
  - Schedule coffee chats with 2-3 people whose work interests you
  - Collaborate on a small project or skill exchange
  - Introduce people who should know each other
  - Consider organizing or co-hosting an event

Ongoing: Maintain and Give
  - Be a reliable presence (consistency builds trust)
  - Refer work to others when you can
  - Celebrate others' wins
  - Ask for help when you need it (vulnerability builds connection)
```

## Hybrid Home-Coworking Strategy

```
Most productive split for many remote workers:

Home Days (2-3 per week):
  - Deep focus work requiring long uninterrupted blocks
  - Tasks where you need your full home setup (dual monitors, etc.)
  - Days with many video calls (better mic/camera at home)
  - Days when coworking space is likely crowded

Coworking Days (2-3 per week):
  - Collaborative work and brainstorming
  - In-person meetings
  - Days when you need energy and motivation from others
  - Networking events or community activities

Optimization tips:
  - Designate specific days for each (consistency helps routine)
  - Keep a minimal desk setup at coworking (reduce transition friction)
  - Use cloud storage exclusively (access files from either location)
  - Sync your calendar to show location to collaborators
```
