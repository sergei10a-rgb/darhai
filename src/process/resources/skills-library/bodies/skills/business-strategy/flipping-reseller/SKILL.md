## Sourcing Strategies

### Thrift Stores and Secondhand Shops
- Visit regularly (new inventory arrives daily)
- Learn each store's restock days and schedule visits accordingly
- Focus on your knowledge areas first, then expand
- Check every section, not just the obvious ones
- Look for quality brands, materials, and construction
- Negotiate bulk discounts when buying multiple items

### Estate Sales and Auctions
- Arrive early for best selection
- Preview items before bidding to assess condition and value
- Set a maximum bid based on your resale research, not emotion
- Estate sales often have progressive discounts on later days
- Build relationships with estate sale companies for early access
- Online estate auctions expand your reach beyond local geography

### Garage and Yard Sales
- Plan routes using online listings the night before
- Arrive at advertised start time for best selection
- Negotiate politely; end-of-sale offers are often accepted at steep discounts
- Look for moving sales (motivated sellers with better items)
- Bring cash in small denominations
- Ask sellers if they have additional items not displayed

### Liquidation and Wholesale
- Liquidation pallets offer volume at low per-unit cost
- Understand that liquidation is a numbers game (some items will be unsellable)
- Start with smaller lots to learn a liquidation source before buying large pallets
- Verify the liquidation company's reputation before purchasing
- Factor in shipping costs for pallets (can be substantial)
- Build relationships with wholesale suppliers for consistent inventory

### Retail Arbitrage
- Scan clearance sections with a pricing research app
- Compare store price to online selling price minus all fees and shipping
- Focus on categories with consistent demand and predictable margins
- Track seasonal clearance patterns by retailer
- Do not overbuy a single item without confirming demand velocity

### Free and Low-Cost Sources
- Curbside finds (furniture, electronics, decor)
- Free listings on community marketplaces
- Dumpster diving near retail stores (legal in most areas, check local laws)
- Networking with friends and family who are decluttering
- Storage unit auctions

## Pricing Research

### Before You Buy: The Research Process
1. Search the item on your target selling platform
2. Filter by "sold" or "completed" listings to see actual selling prices
3. Note the average selling price over the last 30-90 days
4. Check how quickly similar items sell (days on market)
5. Factor in all fees and shipping costs
6. Calculate your target profit margin (aim for 50%+ return on investment)
7. Only buy if the math works after all expenses

### Pricing Formula
Minimum Purchase Price = (Average Sold Price - Platform Fees - Shipping Cost - Packing Cost) / 2

The division by 2 targets a 100% return on investment. Adjust based on your minimum acceptable margin.

### Pricing Strategy by Platform
- Fixed-price platforms: price at or slightly below the lowest comparable active listing
- Auction platforms: start at your minimum acceptable price to attract bidding
- Local platforms: price 10-20% higher than your minimum to allow negotiation room
- Consider best-offer options to capture buyers who want to negotiate

### When to Lower Prices
- Item has not sold in 30 days: reduce by 10-15%
- Still unsold at 60 days: reduce by another 15-20% or move to a different platform
- Seasonal items approaching off-season: discount aggressively to free up capital
- Storage space is at capacity: liquidate slowest-moving inventory

## Platform Selection

### Online Marketplaces with Built-in Audiences
**Auction and fixed-price platforms:**
- Best for unique, collectible, and niche items
- Established buyer base searching for specific items
- Selling fees typically 10-15% of final sale price
- Shipping cost can be included or charged separately

**General e-commerce marketplaces:**
- Massive buyer audience for commodity and brand-name items
- Higher competition but higher volume potential
- Fulfillment services available for hands-off shipping
- Fees vary by category (typically 8-15% plus fulfillment if used)

### Local Selling Platforms
- No shipping hassle (buyer picks up)
- No platform fees on most local platforms
- Best for furniture, large items, and fragile goods
- Faster transaction turnaround
- Requires meeting buyers in person (use safe meeting locations)

### Specialty Platforms
- Fashion-specific platforms for clothing and accessories
- Book-specific platforms for textbook and book selling
- Vintage and antique marketplaces for curated items
- Hobby-specific communities and forums

### Multi-Platform Strategy
- List high-value items on multiple platforms simultaneously
- Use the platform that matches the item category best as primary
- Cross-post with tools that sync listings across platforms
- Delist from other platforms promptly when an item sells

## Listing Optimization

### Photography
- Clean, well-lit photos on a neutral background
- First photo must clearly show the entire item
- Include photos of all angles, details, labels, and any flaws
- Photograph measurements with a ruler or tape measure visible
- Minimum 5 photos per listing; 8-12 for higher-value items
- Consistent photo style builds brand recognition and trust

### Title Optimization
- Include brand, model, size, color, material, and condition
- Use keywords buyers actually search for
- Front-load the most important terms
- Do not use excessive punctuation or all caps
- Research how top sellers title similar items

### Description Best Practices
- State condition honestly and thoroughly
- List dimensions, weight, and materials
- Disclose every flaw with specific description and photo
- Include compatible models or sizes where relevant
- Describe what is included (original box, accessories, manuals)
- Use a consistent template for efficiency

### Condition Grading
- New with tags: original tags attached, never used
- New without tags: unused but tags removed or missing
- Like new: used minimally, no visible signs of wear
- Good: light signs of wear, fully functional
- Fair: noticeable wear but still functional and presentable
- For parts or repair: not fully functional, sold as-is

## Shipping Optimization

### Packaging Fundamentals
- Use the smallest box or mailer that safely fits the item
- Protect items with bubble wrap, paper, or air pillows
- Double-box fragile items with padding between inner and outer box
- Seal all seams with packing tape
- Include a packing slip inside
- Print labels clearly and cover with clear tape to prevent smearing

### Cost Reduction Strategies
- Compare carrier rates for every shipment (prices vary by size and weight)
- Use cubic rate pricing for small, heavy items
- Buy shipping supplies in bulk
- Recycle clean boxes and packing materials from your own deliveries
- Use platform-integrated shipping labels for discounted rates
- Consider flat-rate options for items that fit within weight limits

### Shipping Speed and Tracking
- Ship within your stated handling time (1-2 business days is standard)
- Always use tracked shipping to protect against claims
- Add signature confirmation for items over $100
- Insure high-value shipments
- Upload tracking numbers promptly for buyer peace of mind

### Handling Returns
- Have a clear return policy before your first sale
- Accept returns gracefully on items that were misdescribed (it builds reputation)
- For buyer's remorse returns, follow platform policy
- Inspect returned items immediately upon receipt
- Document condition at receipt in case of disputes

## Category-Specific Tips

### Clothing and Accessories
- Check all seams, zippers, buttons, and fabric for flaws
- Measure key dimensions (chest, length, waist, inseam) and include in listing
- Research brands to know which command premium prices
- Wash or dry-clean before listing (presentation matters)
- Photograph on a dress form or flat-lay consistently

### Electronics
- Test all functions before listing
- Factory reset and remove personal data from previous owners
- Include original accessories and packaging when possible
- Photograph serial numbers and model numbers
- Be specific about what is and is not included
- Disclose cosmetic wear separately from functionality

### Books and Media
- Check for highlighting, writing, water damage, and binding condition
- Look up specific editions and printings (first editions can be valuable)
- Scan ISBNs for instant pricing data
- Textbooks sell best at the start of academic terms
- Bundle related books for higher average sale price

### Furniture and Home Goods
- Clean and make minor repairs before listing
- Photograph in a staged setting when possible
- Provide exact dimensions
- Note weight for items requiring shipping
- Local pickup eliminates shipping risk for large pieces

## Scaling Strategies

### Phase 1: Learn the Ropes ($0-500/month profit)
- Focus on 1-2 categories you know well
- List 5-10 items per week
- Track every purchase, sale, fee, and expense
- Learn what sells and what sits
- Build your sourcing routine

### Phase 2: Build Systems ($500-2,000/month profit)
- Expand to 3-4 categories based on data
- Increase listing volume to 15-30 items per week
- Create listing templates for efficiency
- Establish regular sourcing routes and schedules
- Invest in tools that save time (label printer, photo setup, scanning apps)

### Phase 3: Optimize ($2,000-5,000/month profit)
- Focus on highest-margin categories and eliminate low performers
- Develop reliable wholesale or liquidation sources
- Automate pricing updates and cross-platform listing
- Consider dedicated storage space
- Track inventory with a spreadsheet or inventory management tool

### Phase 4: Scale ($5,000+/month profit)
- Hire help for processing, listing, or shipping
- Negotiate volume discounts with suppliers
- Explore fulfillment service options for high-volume items
- Diversify across multiple platforms for stability
- Consider transitioning top sellers to wholesale or private label

## Financial Tracking

### What to Track Per Item
- Purchase price and date
- Platform listed on and listing date
- Sale price and sale date
- Platform fees
- Shipping cost (charged and actual)
- Packing material cost
- Net profit per item
- Days to sell

### Key Metrics
- Average profit per item
- Average days to sell (inventory turnover)
- Return on investment by category
- Sell-through rate (percentage of inventory that sells within 60 days)
- Total monthly profit after all expenses
- Cost of unsold or dead inventory

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Buying items without researching sold prices first
- Overpaying because of emotional attachment to an item
- Neglecting to account for all fees in profit calculations
- Hoarding inventory instead of pricing to sell
- Listing items with insufficient photos or vague descriptions
- Not disclosing flaws (leads to returns and negative feedback)
- Ignoring shipping costs when calculating profitability
- Diversifying into too many categories before mastering one
- Not tracking financials and assuming you are profitable
- Spending more time sourcing than listing and selling
